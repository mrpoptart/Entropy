# Magic System — Quantified Reference

## Core Principle: Magic Is Energy Conversion

Magic is energy drawn from a planetary reservoir, channeled through the body, and converted into physical effects — heat, force, acceleration of biological processes, structural reinforcement. It follows conservation rules: you're converting magical energy into physical energy, not creating something from nothing.

This means:
- **You can convert energy, not warp reality.** No mind control, no time manipulation, no creating matter from nothing.
- **Channeling requires contact or proximity.** You push energy through something — the ground, air, your hands, another person's body. Distance = exponential inefficiency.
- **Knowledge constrains application.** You can't heal what you don't understand anatomically. You can't reinforce a structure you don't understand architecturally. Power without knowledge is just a blast of unfocused energy.
- **Waste takes the form of heat.** Inefficient channeling bleeds energy as ambient heat. A novice warming a kettle heats up the whole room. A master warms only the water.

---

## Units and Measurement

Magic is measured in **kilojoules (kJ)**, the same unit used for physical energy, because that's what it converts into. People in-world don't use this term — they have their own vocabulary — but for authorial consistency, we map everything to real physics.

**Quick reference points from real-world physics:**
| Effect | Energy Required |
|--------|----------------|
| Warm your hands | 5–10 kJ |
| Heat a cup of tea (250ml) | ~84 kJ |
| Heat a full kettle (1L, to boiling) | ~336 kJ |
| Push open a stuck heavy door | ~0.05–0.1 kJ |
| Shove a person back 2 meters | ~0.5–1 kJ |
| Boost a jump to double height | ~0.5–0.8 kJ |
| Lift a person (80kg) 3 meters | ~2.4 kJ |
| Move a 1-ton boulder 1 meter | ~10 kJ |
| A stick of dynamite | ~4,200 kJ |

**Important:** These are the *output* energy costs — the actual energy that reaches the target. The channeler must spend more than this, with the difference lost as waste heat. How much more depends on their skill (see Efficiency below).

## Daily Pool Budgets by Generation

The pool is a person's daily renewable magical budget. It replenishes overnight (roughly 8 hours of sleep = full recharge). The amount you're born with is largely determined by generational timing — when the planetary reservoir was fuller, pools were larger. However, pools are not perfectly stable within a lifetime: weak-end channelers (young adults, adolescents) experience measurable within-lifetime decline as the planetary reservoir continues draining. A young adult who started at 1,000 kJ/day may find their pool at 900 or 800 within a decade. Elders, drawing from a deeper generational baseline, experience this decline more slowly. This is Maren's trajectory — she's been living with gradual depletion her entire adult life.

| Generation | Age (Book One) | Daily Pool | Relative Power |
|------------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Ancient** (historical) | Dead | ~50,000 kJ | 10x Elder |
| **Elder** | 65–80+ | ~10,000 kJ | Baseline strong |
| **Established** | 45–64 | ~5,000 kJ | Half elder |
| **Middle** | 30–44 | ~2,500 kJ | Quarter elder |
| **Young Adult** | 18–29 | ~1,000 kJ | Tenth of elder |
| **Adolescent** | 12–17 | ~400 kJ | Scraping by |
| **Child** | Under 12 | ~100–200 kJ | Barely functional |

### What These Numbers Feel Like

**An Elder (10,000 kJ/day):**
- Heats their home comfortably all day (~2,000 kJ)
- Heals a few minor injuries at the clinic (~1,500 kJ)
- Powers their share of neighborhood infrastructure maintenance (~1,500 kJ)
- Communicates via resonance objects several times (~500 kJ)
- Cooks meals, warms bath water, handles daily chores (~1,000 kJ)
- Still has ~3,500 kJ in reserve by evening
- *Life feels normal. Comfortable. The crisis is theoretical.*

**A Middle-Generation Adult (2,500 kJ/day):**
- Heats their home, but not as warm, and only the rooms they're in (~800 kJ)
- Can heal a cut or bruise on their kid, but not a broken bone (~200–500 kJ)
- Maintenance shifts at work leave them noticeably tired (~600 kJ)
- Uses resonance sparingly — only important messages (~100 kJ)
- Cooking and daily chores (~400 kJ)
- Maybe 100–600 kJ left by evening, depending on the day
- *Feels the squeeze. Makes trade-offs. Tired by dinner.*

**A Young Adult (1,000 kJ/day):**
- Can heat one room, barely (~300 kJ) — or wears extra layers and skips it
- Healing? Only the smallest cuts. Anything serious means visiting an elder healer (~50–100 kJ)
- Maintenance shifts are exhausting, can only contribute partial shifts (~300 kJ)
- Resonance communication is a luxury — saves it for emergencies (~50 kJ)
- Daily chores take a noticeable chunk (~200 kJ)
- Runs out by late afternoon on a busy day
- *Life is a daily resource management problem. Every expenditure is a choice.*

**An Adolescent (400 kJ/day):**
- Can warm their own hands and maybe a cup of tea
- Can't meaningfully contribute to infrastructure maintenance
- Healing is out of the question — they go to an elder
- Resonance communication costs them 10% of their day
- *Acutely aware of what they can't do. Growing up watching elders do casually what they'll never be able to afford.*

## Efficiency Tiers (Skill as Conversion Rate)

Skill determines how much of your spent energy actually reaches the target versus bleeding off as waste heat. This is *the* reason technique matters — a skilled channeler with a small pool can outperform a sloppy one with a larger pool, because they waste less.

| Skill Level | Efficiency | Description |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Untrained** | ~20% | Brute force. 80% of energy lost as ambient heat. A novice trying to heat a kettle (336 kJ output) must spend ~1,680 kJ — more than a young adult's entire daily pool. |
| **Basic** | ~40% | Functional. Learned the fundamentals. Can do daily tasks without catastrophic waste. Most people operate here. Kettle costs ~840 kJ. |
| **Competent** | ~60% | Practiced. Efficient enough to hold a skilled job. Kettle costs ~560 kJ. |
| **Skilled** | ~75% | Years of dedicated practice. Professional channelers, experienced healers. Kettle costs ~448 kJ. |
| **Master** | ~90% | Exceptional. Decades of refinement. Almost no waste. Kettle costs ~373 kJ. Nearly all energy reaches target. |

### The Efficiency Crossover

This is where the system creates interesting dynamics:

- An **Elder (10,000 kJ) at Basic efficiency (40%)** has an effective output of **4,000 kJ/day**
- A **Young Adult (1,000 kJ) at Master efficiency (90%)** has an effective output of **900 kJ/day**

The elder still wins on raw output by 4x. Skill narrows the gap but can't close it — which is the point. The system is structurally unfair. No amount of practice lets a young person match an elder's raw capacity. It just lets them survive on less.

But for *specific tasks* that require precision over power, the young master can outperform the sloppy elder. A master healer with 1,000 kJ can heal more precisely and with less collateral damage than an elder who dumps 5,000 kJ into a patient with bad technique. The patient just gets cooked.

## Application Costs (What Magic Can Do)

All costs listed are **output energy** — multiply by (1/efficiency) for actual pool cost.

### Thermal Applications
| Application | Output Cost | Notes |
|-------------|------------|-------|
| Warm your hands | 5–10 kJ | Trivial for everyone except small children |
| Heat a cup of tea | ~84 kJ | A young adult's easy task; an adolescent's considered choice |
| Heat a full kettle | ~336 kJ | Easy for elders, a third of a young adult's day |
| Warm a room for an hour | ~500–1,000 kJ | Depends on room size, insulation, outside temp |
| Heat a home all day | ~2,000–4,000 kJ | Comfortable for elders, impossible for young adults alone |
| Ignite kindling | 5–10 kJ (concentrated) | Requires precision — concentrating heat to a point |
| Sustained cooking heat (1 hour) | ~500–800 kJ | More efficient than heating a room because directed |
| Forge-temperature heat on metal | ~2,000–5,000 kJ | Specialized work, elder-tier daily commitment |

### Force / Kinetic Applications
| Application | Output Cost | Notes |
|-------------|------------|-------|
| Shove a stuck door open | 0.05–0.1 kJ | Barely registers on the daily budget |
| Push a person back hard | 0.5–1 kJ | Trivial energy, but requires directing force precisely |
| Boost a jump (2x height) | 0.5–0.8 kJ | Channeled downward through feet |
| Lift yourself 3 meters | ~2.4 kJ | Brief burst, not sustained flight |
| Sustained pressure column (hover, 10 sec) | ~25–50 kJ | Energy-expensive, loud, unstable. Not graceful. |
| Move a 1-ton boulder 1 meter | ~10 kJ | Requires ground contact and knowledge of leverage |
| Collapse a wall | ~50–200 kJ | Depends on material and whether you're working with or against the structure |
| Redirect a falling object (500kg) | ~5–25 kJ | Timing and precision matter more than power |

**On "Levitation":** True sustained flight is prohibitively expensive. Channeling force downward through your feet to launch yourself is feasible (a few kJ per jump). Sustaining a pressure column beneath you for hovering costs ~2.5–5 kJ/second — a young adult burns through their entire daily pool in 3–7 minutes. An elder could sustain it for 30+ minutes but it's loud, rough, and exhausting. Nobody "floats." At best, people rocket-jump or sustain a rough, energy-devouring hover.

### Healing / Biological Applications
| Application | Output Cost | Notes |
|-------------|------------|-------|
| Close a small cut (minutes instead of days) | 50–100 kJ | The bread and butter of healers |
| Reduce swelling/bruising | 30–80 kJ | Accelerating what the body already does |
| Set and mend a broken bone | 500–2,000 kJ | Major procedure. Requires anatomical knowledge. |
| Heal a deep wound (muscle/organ) | 1,000–5,000 kJ | Elder-tier. Multiple sessions for middle gen. |
| Cure an infection (accelerate immune response) | 500–3,000 kJ | Highly variable. Requires understanding the illness. |
| Regrow tissue / major reconstruction | 5,000–15,000 kJ | Ancient-tier. Beyond any living person's single-day capacity. |

**Why healing is expensive:** You're not just applying energy — you're orchestrating complex biological processes. The magical energy acts as both catalyst and fuel for accelerated cell division, tissue repair, immune response. Precision is critical: dump energy without understanding and you accelerate *everything*, including harmful processes. Tumors, runaway inflammation, scar tissue. Bad healing is worse than no healing.

**Why healing is the first thing the decline kills:** A single elder healer can treat maybe 5–10 significant patients per day. A young healer? One, maybe two. The math is devastating for public health.

## The Wellspring — Scale Comparison

Ash's Wellspring reservoir at activation: **~50,000,000 kJ (50 GJ)**

For context:
| Comparison | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| One elder's daily pool | 10,000 kJ |
| One elder's annual pool | ~3,650,000 kJ |
| **The Wellspring** | **50,000,000 kJ** |
| Elder-equivalent days | ~5,000 days (~14 years) |
| Elder-equivalent at reckless use | Could burn through it in months |

This is why it *seems* infinite at first. Even extravagant use — heating entire buildings, spectacular feats of force, healing dozens of people per day — barely dents 50 GJ. Ash has no frame of reference for "running low" because at his scale, the numbers don't feel real.

**But extraordinary feats eat it fast:**

### The Earthquake Problem

A magnitude 4 earthquake releases ~63,000,000 kJ (63 GJ). You can't match that. But you don't need to.

**Counter-vibration approach:** Like noise-canceling headphones. You don't match the total energy of the sound — you produce a precisely timed counter-wave that cancels it locally. Ash channels into the ground through direct physical contact (hands, feet, full body pressed to earth) and produces counter-vibrations that dampen the seismic wave in a localized area.

| Earthquake Intervention | Estimated Cost | Notes |
|------------------------|---------------|-------|
| Protect a single building | 500,000–2,000,000 kJ | Still enormous — 50–200 elder-days |
| Protect a city block | 2,000,000–10,000,000 kJ | 4–20% of total Wellspring |
| Protect a district | 10,000,000–25,000,000 kJ | 20–50% of total Wellspring. Devastating expenditure. |

**This is why the earthquake matters narratively:** It's not just dramatic — it's the moment the Wellspring's finiteness becomes real. Protecting Solathis during the earthquake could cost 20–50% of the total reservoir in a single act. The "infinite" power suddenly has a visible floor.

### Wellspring Depletion Across the Trilogy

| Book | Starting Reservoir | Major Expenditures | Ending Reservoir |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|

## What Magic Cannot Do

These are hard walls, not soft limits:

1. **No mind control or thought reading.** Magic is physical energy. Thoughts are not a physical substrate you can channel into.
2. **No time manipulation.** Energy conversion doesn't bend causality.
3. **No matter creation.** You can move, heat, reshape, reinforce, and accelerate existing matter. You cannot conjure matter from nothing.
4. **No resurrection.** You can heal the living by accelerating biological processes. You cannot reverse death — the processes aren't there to accelerate anymore.
5. **No permanent energy creation.** You can't build a perpetual motion machine with magic. Reinforced structures eventually degrade. Heated stones cool. The channeler must always return.
6. **No action at true distance.** Resonance objects are the sole exception (paired at creation), and even they require the user to channel into them. You cannot reach out and affect something you're not touching or near.

## The Overnight Recharge — How It Works

The pool replenishes during sleep because sleep is when the body's conscious channeling stops and the passive connection to the planetary reservoir can flow unimpeded. Think of it like a well refilling — you can't fill a bucket while you're scooping water out of it.

- ~8 hours of sleep = full recharge to your personal maximum
- Less sleep = partial recharge (roughly proportional)
- You cannot "bank" extra by sleeping more — your personal channel has a maximum flow rate and a maximum capacity
- Illness, extreme stress, or injury can reduce recharge efficiency
- The recharge draws from the planetary reservoir — as the reservoir declines across generations, the maximum pool people are born with gets smaller, but the recharge mechanism itself doesn't break

## Internal Vocabulary (In-World Terms)

**RESOLVED: There is no in-world unit name for magical energy.** Characters do not measure their pools in named units. They use relative and comparative language:
- Fractions: "half my pool," "a third of what I started with"
- Elder-day equivalents: "the equivalent of a hundred elder-days"
- Comparative: "more than an elder produces in three months," "what it cost me to hold the dam"
- Sensory: "I could feel how much was left," "the edges were closer"
- Budget metaphors: "a budget," "a resource," "what's left"

The kJ system stays behind the curtain as the author's consistency tool. Every instance of "kJ" or "kilojoules" in character dialogue, close narration, or Ash's internal monologue must be converted to in-world relative language before a chapter is considered canonical. *(This decision resolves an open question flagged across chapters 14–22.)*

People in this world don't say "kilojoules." They use terms like:
- **Pool** — your daily renewable budget (already established)
- **Spent / burned** — used up your reserves
- **Dry** — out of magic for the day
- **Running hot** — channeling inefficiently, wasting energy as ambient heat
- **Clean channeling** — efficient, precise technique
- **Flooding** — dumping too much energy into a task (brute force approach)
- **The hum** — what it feels like when your pool is full and ready
- **Dead weight** — a person who can't channel (what Ash was called before activation)
