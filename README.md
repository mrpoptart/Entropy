# Entropy

A character-driven fantasy trilogy about power, identity, and what it means to be "special" in a world where magic is dying.

## Open Questions & Areas to Explore

These are bigger-picture questions that don't fit neatly into a single phase — they'll inform decisions across the whole project.

### World & Magic
- What is the *cause* of magic's decline? Is it natural entropy, something someone did, or something being done right now?
- Is the decline reversible? Does anyone believe it is? Does anyone want it to be irreversible?
- What does magic look like in practice — is it rare and dramatic, or woven into everyday life (and therefore its loss is felt everywhere)?
- What are the social/political consequences of magic dying? Who loses power? Who gains it?

### Character & Theme
- What does "special" mean in this world? Is it about magical ability, lineage, destiny, or something Ash constructs for himself?
- Is Ash actually special, or does he just believe he is (or need to be)?
- How does the sister's relationship to "specialness" mirror or contrast Ash's?
- What is the emotional core of the trilogy — is it ultimately about acceptance, transformation, grief, or something else?

### Structure & Craft
- What is the target word count per book? (Affects pacing and outline granularity)
- Is this aimed at a specific market (YA, adult, literary fantasy, commercial fantasy)?
- Are there structural experiments planned (non-linear timeline, unreliable narrator, epistolary sections)?

## Principles

- **Characters first, plot second.** The story exists to pressure-test these people. Plot serves character, not the other way around.
- **Theme is the backbone.** Every character, scene, and subplot should connect back to the central questions about power, identity, and what it means to be special.
- **Plan thoroughly, draft freely.** The planning phase should be detailed enough that drafting feels like discovery within a structure, not wandering.
- **No placeholder characters.** Every named character should have a reason to exist that connects to the theme. If they don't, cut them or find their purpose.
- **The magic system serves the metaphor.** Magic dying isn't just a plot device — it's the lens through which every thematic question is examined.
