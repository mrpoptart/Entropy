# Character Index

Quick reference for all characters in the trilogy. Each major character has their own detailed file in this directory.

## Major Characters
- **The Protagonist** *(name TBD)* — Male, early-to-mid 20s. Born without magic in a world that runs on it. Becomes a Wellspring — the most powerful channeler alive, with a massive but finite reservoir. Outwardly cheerful, privately grieving. Brilliant, physically capable, deeply relational. The trilogy follows his journey from defining himself by power to finding identity beyond it. *(See: protagonist.md)*
- **The Sister** *(name TBD)* — Female, approximately two years younger. The protagonist's closest mirror. An overachiever driven by unspoken guilt over having the magic her brother didn't get. Competent, controlled, unable to ask for help. Her arc parallels the protagonist's as she faces her own version of losing the thing she built her identity on. *(See: sister.md)*

## Supporting Characters (To Be Developed)
- **The Mother** — Fierce protector who defined herself by sacrificing for her magicless son. Faces identity crisis when he no longer needs her.
- **The Father** — Proud but quietly terrified. Tried to build his son's strength and independence. Fears he's failed as a father.
- **The Childhood Friend** *(name TBD)* — Female, same age as the protagonist. Grew up on the same street, competent channeler with a normal trickle. The friend who never treated his condition as the most interesting thing about him — and the only person who refuses to treat the Wellspring as the most interesting thing, either. Represents the road not taken (ordinary life) and serves as the truth-teller (the person who knew him before and won't pretend she didn't). Their friendship is strained by his drift into power and tested by reconnection. *(See: childhood-friend.md)*
- **The Tinkerer Mentor** *(name TBD)* — Male, late 60s to early 70s. Runs a workshop in one of the city's poorer districts, building and experimenting with non-magical mechanisms. Has a strong elder-generation trickle but chose decades ago to stop relying on magic — he saw the decline coming and built a life that doesn't depend on channeling. Patient, dry-humored, expresses care through attention and shared work. The protagonist's philosophical anchor and the living proof that identity beyond power is possible. *(See: mentor.md)*
- **The Patron** *(name TBD)* — Female, late 50s to early 60s. A senior figure in the city's civic administration — politically astute, strategically generous, and deeply invested in the institutional systems that hold the city together. Took the protagonist on as an aide for reasons both sincere and calculated. When the Wellspring activates, her protective instincts and institutional logic merge into an escalating need to direct and manage his power. Not a villain — a competent leader whose framework for understanding people as functions within a system becomes the primary antagonistic force of Book One. *(See: patron.md)*

## Antagonists (To Be Developed)
- **Book One Antagonist concept:** Someone who wants to control the Wellspring as an asset
- **Book Two Antagonist concept:** Someone trying to stop the magical decline by force, making things worse
- **Book Three Antagonist concept:** Despair and nihilism embodied — a movement or leader who argues there's no point in building for a magicless future
