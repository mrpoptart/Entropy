# Word Count Estimates

## Book One — "The Brightest Fire"

**Estimated total: ~90,000–100,000 words**

The outline contains 22 chapters across 3 acts.

| Act | Chapters | Count | Est. Words/Chapter | Subtotal |
|-----|----------|-------|--------------------|----------|
| Act One (Ordinary World & Activation) | Ch 1–4 | 4 | ~4,500 | ~18,000 |
| Act Two (The Ascent) | Ch 5–17 | 13 | ~4,000 | ~52,000 |
| Act Three (The Reckoning) | Ch 18–22 | 5 | ~5,000 | ~25,000 |
| **Total** | | **22** | | **~95,000** |

### Reasoning

- **Act One** chapters are dense with world-building and character establishment — these tend to run longer as they set up the magic system, Ash's daily life, family dynamics, the workshop, and the Wellspring activation.
- **Act Two** has 13 chapters covering the honeymoon, drift, and earthquake sequence. Some are action-heavy (shorter), some are tonal/relational (mid-length). The earthquake chapters (15–17) are set pieces that will run long.
- **Act Three** chapters are emotionally dense and pivotal — the math scene, the break with Dorenne, the walk home. These tend to run longer per chapter due to internal weight.

### Genre comparison

For adult character-driven fantasy, ~95,000 words is solidly in range:
- Adult fantasy debut novels typically run 80,000–120,000 words
- Character-driven literary fantasy tends toward 85,000–110,000
- Comparable titles: *The Goblin Emperor* (~100K), *Piranesi* (~70K), *The Curse of Chalion* (~110K)

**Verdict: Book One is long enough.** It has room for the world-building and emotional depth this story needs without risking bloat.
