## Criterion-by-Criterion Analysis

### 1. Voice & Style

**Carver — 9/10**

Carver is the cleanest match to the style guide throughout. The opening — "The workshop smelled the same" — is a single declarative sentence that dives into scene without ceremony, exactly per the style guide's instruction to open chapters in scene rather than summary. The baseline prose stays in medium third across the chapter, pulling into closer territory only when warranted: the italicized thought *That's not a small trickle. That can't be.* is well-placed, short, and arrives at a moment of genuine realization. Verbs are precise — "ran his thumbnail along it," "He seated a bolt and gave it two turns" — doing real observational work without reaching for effect. Dialogue tags are workmanlike and appropriate. The chapter avoids exposition dumps: the pump's purpose and the district's infrastructure problem are conveyed through Haran's first lines, already at work.

One minor issue: the phrase "permanent archaeology of projects" in the opening workshop description risks sliding into the kind of language the style guide identifies as a performance — the author admiring their own metaphor. It's a small slip in an otherwise clean draft.

**Hearthkeeper — 8/10**

Hearthkeeper opens with a longer first paragraph and more atmospheric scene-setting, which slows the arrival into action. The description of the workshop is well-observed, but the style guide specifies two or three strong sensory details per new location — Hearthkeeper gives closer to five, and not all of them earn their place. "A slow drift of sawdust that spun in the current from the door Ash had opened" is vivid but lingers longer than it needs to. The prose picks up once Haran speaks, and the later sections — particularly the revelation passage and the "Your hands would be useful" beat — are extremely clean. The italicized interiority is used frequently but almost always correctly: *He's not being careful with his trickle. He's been doing this all day.* and *He's not doing a little. He's doing everything at the smallest possible scale.* land with precision. The chapter loses a point because it opens slightly over-described and because one passage — the extended narrator summary of Ash's post-revelation understanding at lines 225–229 — crosses from free indirect discourse into something that feels like the narrator explaining the insight rather than Ash experiencing it.

**Lyricist — 8/10**

Lyricist opens from outside the workshop, following Ash through the district approach. This is a structural choice that departs from the style guide's "dive in" instruction for chapter openings — the first two paragraphs are transitional rather than active. The opening line — "The district smelled like charcoal and wet stone, and Ash arrived buzzing" — is energetic and shows character voice, which partially compensates. But the style guide is explicit: "the first line of each chapter should orient the reader in scene." Being outside the workshop and on approach is not quite the same as being in scene. Once inside, Lyricist demonstrates strong prose economy, particularly in the failure sequence. The three * * * breaks in the first half of the chapter are used more frequently than in either other draft, and one of them — the break after the fused joint — is not earned; the chapter could have continued through to the valve without a break and gained momentum. Lyricist also has the most explicit summary ending: "They weren't at all the same / He thought about that all the way home." This tells the reader what conclusion to draw from the chapter rather than trusting them to draw it.

### 2. Scene Execution

**Carver — 8/10**

All eight beats are present, with strong dramatization of most. Beat 1 (arrival/buzzing) is the weakest — the press event is mentioned only in a brief exchange with Haran ("Press event run long?" / "Three hours.") and the buzz Ash felt is not dramatized through his body or interiority. We are told that Ash "lasted about thirty seconds" before intervening, but not given the felt sense of the reservoir's pull — which is important because the chapter brief specifies he arrives wanting to help and the urge to use the power should be vivid. Beats 2–4 (the three mistakes and the valve failure) are the most precisely executed of any draft: the mistakes are individuated — the collar fuses, the rod stiffens, the pipe work-hardens, the valve housing cracks — each with a specific mechanical consequence, and the tinkerers' reactions are shown through glances and adjusted posture rather than named. Beat 5 (teaching) is strong. Beat 6 (revelation) is excellent — the best realization passage of any draft. Beats 7–8 (still half-built, departure, warning) are efficiently done. The ending is clean.

**Hearthkeeper — 9/10**

Hearthkeeper is the most complete beat-for-beat execution of the brief. Beat 1 is fully dramatized: we feel the buzzing directly ("the thing behind his ribs that had been buzzing since this morning... began, almost reluctantly, to settle") and the warmth of the workshop is given as an immediate physical grounding for Ash's state. The three failure incidents (fused flange, rigid pipe, fused bayonet seal) are distinct and the consequences of each are explicitly different — "you built them for maintenance," "the pipe was done" — which demonstrates understanding of the chapter's central thematic point about downstream cost. Beat 3 (valve failure) unfolds over a full scene section and includes the most detail of any draft about what goes wrong mechanically and why. Beat 4 (Haran observes and takes over) is clean and unmoralistic. Beat 5 (teaching) includes the demonstration with the gear blank that is unique to this draft and is the best physicalization of micro-channeling in any version. Beat 6 (revelation) arrives organically from observation. Beats 7–8 are the only place Hearthkeeper slips: the departure exchange is the weakest version of the "remember what the answer is" dialogue because Haran is cleaning tools while it happens, and the action slightly dilutes the line's weight.

**Lyricist — 8/10**

Lyricist has all eight beats but executes them at slightly different depths. The arrival beat is the most fully dramatized (best of any draft for Beat 1 — the "blood still humming like a plucked string" and the specific detail of calibrating his handshake to not break anything are strong). The failure sequence uses three beats divided by scene breaks, which gives each incident appropriate space, but the bayonet seal moment is slightly rushed — Ash recognizes what he's done immediately ("he knew it as he felt it set"), which is accurate, but the sequence between recognition and Haran's response is short, and the specific beat of Haran saying nothing — just making a notation in his schematic — is underplayed relative to its importance. The teaching section has the best iteration of "micro-pulse" as a term and concept, and the scrap pile exercise is unique to Lyricist and is genuinely useful dramatization. Beat 6 (revelation) is competent but slightly over-explained — see Thread Delivery below. Beat 8 (warning) is the best of any draft: Haran delivers the final lines while threading, the physical action of the handle turning giving the dialogue a rhythm that is exactly right, and Ash's encounter with the flagstone on the way out is a beautifully placed piece of habit-as-metaphor.

### 3. Emotional Precision

**Carver — 8/10**

Carver handles the comedy-to-failure arc cleanly. The three mistakes build with appropriate acceleration, and the tinkerers' reactions are precise — glances and adjusted expression rather than named reactions. The emotional peak is the revelation scene, and Carver executes it well: *He could have maintained a district* lands as a thought rather than a statement, the quiet weight is appropriate. The warmth of the ending — "You chose this" / "Neither did you. Yet." — is the most emotionally resonant version of that exchange in any draft. The correction "Yet" is gentle and affectionate and Haran-accurate, and Ash accepting it without pushback shows his relationship with Haran correctly. What the draft undersells is the comedy of the opening: the arrival should be warmer, slightly lighter — the chapter brief specifies "warm comedy" as the opening register, and while Carver gets the workshop atmosphere right, Ash's energy in the room is muted. His excitement from the press event is not felt, only reported.

**Hearthkeeper — 9/10**

Hearthkeeper's emotional arc is the most faithfully executed. The opening warmth is earned through physical grounding (the buzzing thing behind his ribs settling as he enters the workshop), and the comedy of the failure sequence is present without being overplayed — the tinkerers' glances do real work, and Haran's "Fused" (his only word after the first mistake) is perfectly dry. The failure at the valve seat is the most detailed of any draft and the only one where the reader genuinely feels Ash's frustration building: "He picked up the disc again. He picked it up again." The repetition is Ash's stubbornness and his hands' certainty fighting the power, and it lands. The revelation moment is the best awe beat of any draft (see Best Passages). The warmth of the ending is present, though slightly weaker than Carver's at the "You chose this" beat — Hearthkeeper doesn't include that exchange. The departing warmth instead comes from "Your hands would be useful" (not the Wellspring, the hands) and the physical detail of Ash's hands on the components during the final working section.

**Lyricist — 8/10**

The emotional arc is present but somewhat externalized. Lyricist relies more on Ash's stated internal reactions and slightly less on physical rendering: "He stood back. He looked at what he'd done." and "A battering ram wearing gloves and trying very hard to be careful" describe rather than show the emotional experience. The "battering ram" image is clever but it is the narrator's description of Ash's sensation, not Ash's own sensory experience. The scrap pile section is the most uplifting moment — watching Ash stop the thread before it runs away and then pocket the gear casing, intending to practice at home, is genuinely warm — but Lyricist's ending undermines this warmth with over-explanation. "They weren't at all the same. He thought about that all the way home." The chapter has already earned this thought; spelling it out weakens it.

### 4. Character Authenticity

**Carver — 9/10**

Ash: The analytical mind is precisely rendered in the failure sequence. Each mistake is followed by Ash's immediate mechanical assessment of what went wrong — "The thread surfaces...had micro-fused. The collar would thread on. It would hold. But it wouldn't come off again" — which is exactly how Ash's intellect works per his profile: he's brilliant, he diagnoses problems, he sees the downstream consequences. The internal contradiction of *That works fine. It'll hold for years.* immediately after the first mistake is perfect — it's the willful blindness the profile identifies, the way he talks himself past the problem. Haran is consistently himself: his dialogue is sparse, he teaches by doing and by asking questions ("What were you feeling for?"), and the gentle "Yet" at the end is exactly the affectionate correction of a man who cares about Ash and won't pretend otherwise.

**Hearthkeeper — 9/10**

Ash is well-rendered in the moment of assuming Haran is conserving a weak trickle — "the assumption you didn't examine, the way you didn't examine the furniture in a room you'd lived in since childhood" — this is Ash's blind spot operating exactly as described in his profile: smart enough to see most things, capable of willful blindness when he's gotten used to seeing something a certain way. The social intelligence is present in the way he quietly adjusts his role after the revelation, fetching the component Haran will need next without being asked. Haran's "Your hands would be useful" (not the Wellspring, the hands) is the cleanest and most character-accurate version of that beat in any draft. The one authenticity issue is Haran's extended speech beginning "Your hands are the right instrument" — which is slightly more explicit and analytical than Haran typically speaks. His profile specifies that his wisdom comes out sideways, in comments about gears that turn out to be about life. This line is the most direct of any speech in the chapter.

**Lyricist — 8/10**

Ash's characterization is solid but one moment slips: the explicit summary "He had done it faster. He had done most of it worse" is good prose but a slightly theatrical summation rather than something that emerges from Ash's interiority. Haran is well-rendered throughout, and the scrap pile exercise shows Haran's patience accurately. However, Lyricist has Haran say "Your reservoir is — larger than anything I know how to account for. Controlling small outputs from something that large is like trying to whisper in a hurricane." This is too large a metaphor, too self-aware as a statement. Haran's metaphors are concrete and mechanical. "You don't use a hammer on glass" (present in all three drafts) is the correct register: short, physical, tool-based. "Whisper in a hurricane" is poetic and sounds wrong in Haran's mouth.

### 5. Thread Delivery

**Carver — 8/10**

The chapter's thematic requirements are delivered, but some are more present than others. Haran's philosophy (radical efficiency, not abstinence) is shown through the revelation scene and demonstrated physically throughout. The pump as worldview is present in Haran's description of it — "the whole assembly was built to be dismantled" — and in the observation at the end that the collar and rod will cost someone else an afternoon. Ash's blind spot is established but the chapter leans more heavily on mechanical consequences than on Ash's incomplete understanding of his own cost — the "he didn't think about that afternoon belonging to someone" beat is explicit and useful, but it comes as narration rather than Ash's own thought. The lesson-smuggled-without-a-speech requirement is well met: Haran never gives a lecture. The departure warning ("Remember what the answer is") could land with more weight.

**Hearthkeeper — 9/10**

Thread delivery is the strongest of any draft. The pump-as-philosophy is embedded from the beginning: "built to be dismantled — every joint a fitting, every component accessible, every piece designed to be replaced by whoever came next without needing to know who had built it." This arrives while Ash is already at the bench looking at the pump, from his own observation rather than from narration. The downstream cost of Ash's interventions is the most fully articulated of any draft. Ash's blind spot is rendered exactly correctly: "He had helped, genuinely, with three parts of it in the last five minutes... He felt good about this, mostly." The "mostly" is precise. The final paragraph ("He probably would not notice what the fused bayonet mount cost the person who needed to clean the inlet, three years from now") is the boldest editorial move in any draft — the narrator stepping in to tell us what Ash won't think about — and it works because it delivers the thematic point cleanly at the end, not as a moral lesson but as a simple fact.

**Lyricist — 8/10**

Thread delivery is competent across the board but the revelation is the most explicitly summarized: "Not abstinence. Not restraint. Mastery wearing the clothing of ordinary." This is three short sentences that decode the thematic content directly for the reader. Hearthkeeper and Carver both allow this understanding to build from accumulating detail and let the reader hold it without being told what to call it. "Mastery wearing the clothing of ordinary" is a strong line — but it belongs earlier in the accumulation, as one of Ash's emerging thoughts during observation, not as the narrator's summary. The pump-as-philosophy is present but the most thinly drawn of any draft.

### 6. Worldbuilding Integration

**Carver — 9/10**

The magic system is shown entirely through use. The distinction between Ash's reservoir (vast, blunt, un-trained) and Haran's trickle (small but precise, decades-trained) is demonstrated through action and concrete detail: the efficiency tables Ash half-memorized, the lamp lit cleanly from half a meter, the specific absence of waste heat. The district is shown through function rather than description — the four people on channeling rotation, the pump broken for eight months, "that's how it goes." No exposition dumps.

**Hearthkeeper — 9/10**

Equal to Carver in most respects. The micro-pulse demonstration with the gear blank is the clearest teaching moment for how the magic system works — "a thumbnail of heat in its exact center" is concrete and specific enough that the reader understands exactly what level of control is being described without needing numbers. The district detail is slightly richer than Carver's. The workshop texture is the fullest of any draft because Hearthkeeper builds Haran's ambient channeling gradually and then reveals it as the source of the room's atmosphere, making the worldbuilding double as revelation.

**Lyricist — 8/10**

Solid worldbuilding integration, weakened slightly by the approach sequence outside the workshop, which describes the district's infrastructure through summary rather than through Ash actively doing something in the district. The magic system is otherwise shown cleanly through use, and the scrap pile exercise is good embedded worldbuilding — it demonstrates that tinkerers have scrap specifically for practice, which tells us something about how magic learning works.

### 7. Continuity

**Carver — 9/10**

Tight continuity throughout. The lattice accidents and the apartment sequence from Chapter 7 are echoed correctly: Ash's instinct to help with his hands is still present, the Wellspring still reacts to intent rather than deliberate action, and the cost of using it remains unfelt. The phrasing "The reservoir behind his ribs hadn't shifted" at multiple points mirrors Chapter 7's established language. The closing image — Ash standing with his hand on the interface — is a direct thematic rhyme with the end of Chapter 7. No continuity errors noted.

**Hearthkeeper — 8/10**

Good continuity overall, with one notable issue. Hearthkeeper's closing walk through the district has Ash channel warmth into his coat: "He channeled a small warmth into his coat — not thinking about it, barely noticing." This cuts against Chapter 7's established pattern: Ash cannot interact with channeling interfaces without consequences, and his attempts to channel small amounts consistently exceed intent. A casual, successful channeling into his coat — even framed as "barely noticing" — suggests more accidental competence than Chapter 7 has established. This is a small but real continuity problem.

**Lyricist — 9/10**

Clean continuity. The flagstone detail — "the flagstone that would tip under his left boot, and he stepped over it without thinking" — is the best embodiment of the chapter's thematic relationship to accumulated physical knowledge. Lyricist correctly avoids giving Ash any accidental channeling successes that would contradict Chapter 7's established control issues. No continuity errors noted.

## Best Passages

### Carver

**1. The revelation**

> "He'd been watching Haran work for years... He'd always read those moments as utility — a man with a small trickle using it efficiently for small tasks. / He looked again. / Haran's hand on the fitting. The warmth that came from it — controlled, specific, limited exactly to the thread and not a millimeter beyond. No waste heat in the air, no ambient warmth. The energy had gone where he directed it and nowhere else."

The understanding builds in increments — Ash looks, thinks, looks again, thinks more — and each increment adds a concrete observation. *He could have maintained a district* arrives as a thought in Ash's voice without narrator amplification. Haran's response — the small softening around the eyes, then back to work — is perfectly Haran. "Neither did you. Yet." is the emotional high point of any draft.

**2. The first failure**

> "The collar turned. More than turned — released completely, the threads giving all at once. / He set it down, satisfied. Then he looked at it. / The brass was slightly darker where he'd touched it..."

The structure is exactly right: Ash solves the problem, feels satisfaction, and then the analytical mind catches up. The interval between "satisfied" and "Then he looked at it" is where the whole failure sequence lives.

**3. Haran's teaching dialogue**

> "The valve work. When you calibrated it by hand, before. What were you feeling for?" / "Back-pressure. The cone seating. There's a specific resistance when it's right — you can feel the whole seat load through the stem." / "What else?"

The technique is Socratic — Haran asks, Ash answers, and through answering Ash articulates the knowledge he already has. "What else?" is a Haran line: patient, continuing, not offering the answer. The conclusion ("So your hands already know what precision feels like") arrives as the logical endpoint of what Ash has just said, not as wisdom imposed on the scene.

---

### Hearthkeeper

**1. The revelation passage**

> "He had assumed — without examining the assumption, the way you didn't examine the furniture in a room you'd lived in since childhood — that the warmth Haran kept in the workshop was modest because modest was all he had... / None of it was large. All of it was constant."

"None of it was large. All of it was constant" is the best single sentence in any of the three drafts. Six words with no ornamentation. The distinction between "not doing a little" and "doing everything at the smallest possible scale" is the clearest articulation of Haran's philosophy. "Not quite a smile. The quality that a smile might be made from" is the most precise description of an expression in any version.

**2. The gear blank demonstration**

> "A small warmth bloomed in the exact center of the brass. A circle, barely larger than a thumbnail, a few degrees of difference — Ash could see it in the way the light caught the surface, the faint difference in reflection where the metal had accepted heat. Then it cooled. A second later, another brief, precise bloom, slightly off-center."

This passage does something none of the other drafts do: it shows what mastery looks like at the micro level through physical observation. "A listening rather than a doing" is the most precise description of what micro-channeling is in contrast to what Ash is doing.

**3. The end of the failure sequence**

> "He had done it faster. He had done most of it worse."

Two sentences, earning their compression after three scene sections of build.

### Lyricist

**1. The departure**

> "Ash opened the door. Cold air from the lane — charcoal and wet stone and the distant metal smell of a district where things were built rather than bought. The light was going out of the afternoon..."

The lane smell returning from the chapter's opening, the flagstone stepped over without thinking, the gear casing in the coat pocket — all earned callbacks. "A district where things were built rather than bought" is strong world-characterization in a subordinate clause. Haran threading while he speaks gives the dialogue rhythm and shows the work continuing.

**2. The arrival**

> "He couldn't help it. The press event had been three hours ago... and he'd walked out into the afternoon with his blood still humming like a plucked string. / The Wellspring didn't care that he was trying to walk normally."

The best arrival of any draft. "His blood still humming like a plucked string" renders the buzz as physical sensation. "The Wellspring didn't care that he was trying to walk normally" establishes the chapter's comic register immediately and is accurately Ash's voice — dry, self-aware, resigned.

**3. The scrap pile**

> "The third time he stopped in time, he looked up. Haran was watching him with an expression Ash recognized from years of work in this room — the look Haran wore when something was going roughly the way it was supposed to go, which was different from the look he wore when something was going well. Progress was not the same as success. Haran's face understood the difference."

"Progress was not the same as success. Haran's face understood the difference." Strong articulation of Haran's teaching philosophy through Ash's learned observation — the correct use of limited POV.

## Weakest Passages

### Carver

**1. Arrival beat** — Ash's buzz from the press event is reported, not felt. "He stood in the doorway for a moment and let it settle over him" is vague. The chapter brief calls for warm comedy opening; Carver's opening is warm but not comedic because Ash's energized state is not present to be deflated.

**2. The efficiency table numbers** — Magic system quantification risks the "numbers behind the curtain" problem. The observation can be made entirely through what Ash sees (zero waste heat, flame at the wick only) without requiring him to reference percentages.

**3. Narrated summary near the end** — "He hadn't thought about that afternoon, five years from now, belonging to someone" explicitly names the theme after the chapter has already established it. Remove in favor of the physical facts already shown.

---

### Hearthkeeper

**1. Opening simile** — "A word you'd almost forgotten, the tune of a song you knew before you knew the words." Over-reaching in an opening paragraph that should stay at baseline. One clean sensory image is enough.

**2. Haran's direct teaching speech** — "You already know precision. You just need to teach the power what your hands already know" is delivered as a clean thesis statement. Haran teaches by asking, not by declaring. Replace with the Socratic version from Carver.

**3. The coat-warming detail** — Continuity problem with Chapter 7. Ash channeling warmth into his coat "without thinking" implies accidental competence not yet established. Remove.

---

### Lyricist

**1. The summary ending** — "They weren't at all the same. / He thought about that all the way home." The repetition of "They weren't" underlines a point already made. The chapter's meaning should rest with the pump still half-built, not with Ash explicitly thinking about the lesson.

**2. "Battering ram wearing gloves"** — Narrator's metaphor rather than Ash's sensory experience. Reaches for effect in a scene that should stay in the physical register of hands and metal.

**3. Haran's "hurricane" metaphor** — "Controlling small outputs from something that large is like trying to whisper in a hurricane." Poetic in a way that is wrong for Haran's voice. His metaphors are concrete and tool-based.

## Recommended Path

### Primary Recommendation: Build from Hearthkeeper

Hearthkeeper is the strongest complete draft. It executes the most scene beats, handles the emotional arc most faithfully, articulates the chapter's themes without over-explaining them, and contains the chapter's best single sentence ("None of it was large. All of it was constant") alongside the best rendering of the revelation's emotional weight. The gear blank demonstration is unique to this draft and is the most effective teaching moment for the magic system. The thematic endpoint — "He probably would not notice what the fused bayonet mount cost the person who needed to clean the inlet, three years from now" — is the boldest and most honest version of Ash's blind spot. It should stay.

### Passages to Transplant

**From Carver — Beat 1 opening exchange:**
"Press event run long?" / "Three hours." / "Hm." Carver's version of Haran's reaction to the press event world — asking without turning around, getting a number, responding with nothing — says more about Haran than any simile. Use instead of Hearthkeeper's "You look like a kettle that needs to blow off steam."

**From Carver — The teaching dialogue:**
Replace Hearthkeeper's "You already know precision... you just need to teach the power what your hands already know" with Carver's Socratic version: "The valve work. When you calibrated it by hand, before. What were you feeling for?" — letting Ash answer himself into the insight. The conclusion line can stay but should arrive as the endpoint of Ash's own articulation.

**From Lyricist — The arrival energy:**
Replace Hearthkeeper's opening paragraph's extended simile with the physical arrival beat from Lyricist: "his blood still humming like a plucked string" and the calibrated handshake. Compress to two or three sentences.

**From Lyricist — The departure:**
Replace Hearthkeeper's departure (Haran cleaning tools while speaking) with Lyricist's threading version. Haran threading while he speaks gives the dialogue rhythm and shows the work continuing. Add the flagstone detail and the gear casing in the coat pocket as the chapter's final physical beat before Ash starts home.

### What NOT to Pull

- Lyricist's ending (the explicit summary) — trust the reader
- Hearthkeeper's coat-warming detail — continuity problem
- Lyricist's "hurricane" metaphor in Haran's mouth — wrong register
- Hearthkeeper's opening extended simile — over-reaching for the chapter's baseline

## Revision Notes for the Foundation Draft (Hearthkeeper)

1. **Replace the opening paragraph's extended simile.** Cut "a word you'd almost forgotten, the tune of a song you knew before you knew the words." Render Ash's return to the workshop through his physical reaction (the buzzing settling, the specific relaxation in his shoulders).

2. **Dramatize Beat 1 — Ash's arrival energy.** Use Lyricist's physical details (blood humming, calibrated handshake) compressed to two sentences. The comedy of the opening requires Ash to arrive warm and slightly self-important so that his failures land as deflation rather than mere frustration.

3. **Revise Haran's teaching speech to arrive Socratically.** Replace "You already know precision. You just need to teach the power what your hands already know" with Carver's question-based version. The insight should be the same; Haran should ask, not declare.

4. **Remove the coat-warming detail in the departure.** The "essentially nothing" exchange already covers the reservoir's indifference to small expenditures. The coat-warming creates a continuity problem with Chapter 7 and is redundant.

5. **Replace Hearthkeeper's departure exchange with Lyricist's threading version.** The final lines need the physical action of work continuing. Add the flagstone and the gear casing in the pocket as the chapter's final physical detail before Ash starts home — they close the chapter on accumulated knowledge and forward intention.
