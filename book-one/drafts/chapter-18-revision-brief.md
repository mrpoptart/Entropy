# Revision Brief — Chapter 18: "Edges"

## Critical Issues (must fix)

**1. "Since the end of Chapter 17" — manuscript artifact in prose**
Line 31: "the quality of it had shifted again since the end of Chapter 17." This is a drafting note that was never replaced with an in-world time marker. Characters do not think in chapter numbers. Fix: replace with any temporal in-world reference — "since the night on the overlook," "since the reconstruction plan began," "in the weeks since the earthquake." (Voice Editor, Arc Guardian, Thematic Compass, Continuity Tracker, Convergence Tracker — flagged by all five.)

**2. `chapter-11.md` line 81 — Dalla/Verren naming conflict, fifth consecutive flag**
"Verren, who handled the scheduling" in chapter-11.md remains unresolved. Every chapter from Ch15 onward uses "Dalla." This has been flagged as mandatory in the Ch11, Ch15, Ch16, and Ch17 revision briefs and never applied. Ch18 does not introduce a new instance, but ch19 cannot be drafted cleanly until the canonical file is corrected. Fix: change "Verren" to "Dalla" in `chapter-11.md` line 81. One word. (Continuity Tracker.)

**3. "Verin's crew" — new name creates three-name cluster**
Line 101: "Verin's crew has been waiting since ten." "Verin" appears to be a field crew chief, distinct from the scheduling aide. But the name is phonetically close to "Verren" and lands in a chapter where the Dalla/Verren conflict is still unresolved in the canonical file, creating a three-name cluster (Verren/Dalla/Verin). Fix: add a single clarifying identifier — "Verin, the eastern wall foreman" or "the wall crew" — that distinguishes this person from the aide name cluster. (Continuity Tracker.)

**4. The Maren amplification does not fire — load-bearing for Ch19**
The convergence map's cross-thread amplification is explicit: in Ch18, Maren's return should amplify Thread 1. Her daily finite-pool budget-balancing should make Ash's newly perceived reservoir edges feel personal and intimate — she is the world's decline at intimate scale. The draft registers the information correctly: "spending her pool, her small and precise and diminishing pool... the way everyone except him did." But Ash immediately deflects to Dorenne's chart before the disproportion registers as devastating. The Ch19 math payoff — "she's been living with this math her whole life" — will land as fresh information rather than inevitable arrival if Ch18 doesn't strike the note first. Fix: after "the way everyone except him did," extend by three to four beats. Hold both pictures simultaneously — her rationed lifetime against his ocean with its new geometry of edges — long enough for the grotesque asymmetry to register. Then let him push it away, consistent with his avoidance pattern. The push-away is correct; the hit must come first. (Convergence Tracker — highest priority; Arc Guardian concurs.)

**5. POV breach — mother's unspoken wants named as fact**
A passage presents the mother's interior state directly: what she wanted, what she was holding back, what she had decided not to say. Ash cannot know this. Fix: anchor in what Ash observes — her expression, her hands, the quality of the pause — and frame interior content as inference rather than fact. The restraint is visible; the specific content of what she is restraining belongs to her interiority. (Voice Editor, Continuity Tracker.)

**6. "The limits were real" — Ch19's conclusion stated in Ch18**
The final night scene has Ash explicitly articulating "The limits were real" as a conclusion. This is the landing point of Ch19's math scene, not Ch18's. Ch18's job is to make the edges perceptible and carry the unease of awareness without the precision of knowledge; Ch19 translates that awareness into numbers and certainty. Fix: pull the night scene back to threshold — he feels the edges, he knows something has changed, but the phrase "the limits were real" or its equivalent should not appear until Ch19. End Ch18 on awareness, not conclusion. (Pace Keeper, Convergence Tracker.)

**7. "Winter was months away" — season timing inconsistent**
Line 163: "the thick, peppery kind she made in winter, though winter was months away." Ch15 establishes the earthquake in early autumn; Ch18 is six-plus weeks into reconstruction, placing it in mid-to-late autumn. From mid-autumn, winter is weeks away, not months. Fix: "the cold-season kind" or "the kind she made when the days shortened" preserves the emotional function without the calendar error. (Continuity Tracker.)

**8. "Thin" — bodily cost ambiguity contradicts magic mechanics**
Lines 175–179: the mother says Ash looks thin twice, with no clarifying context. The outline states explicitly (Ch17 entry): "He's tired in a way he hasn't been before — not physically (Wellspring power carries no bodily cost)." A reader taking the observation at face value will conclude channeling is draining Ash's body, contradicting a core magic-system rule. Fix: one brief beat attributing the thinness to skipped meals or long deployment hours — consistent with character behavior — rather than leaving the cause ambiguous. (Continuity Tracker, World Keeper.)

**9. "Toreth" / "Tereth" near-homophone**
Ch18 line 211 introduces Toreth, Haran's tinkerer neighbor who taught Ash to build shelves. Ch17 uses Tereth for the man in the Verengate apartment (Sera's husband, father of baby Ashyn). Two characters, one vowel apart, in the same act, in the same emotional register of "warm people Ash has a relationship with." Fix: rename one before Ch19. The Ch18 tinkerer neighbor should be renamed (this chapter is less canonical than Ch17 at this point); options: "Torel," "Areth," "Comyn." (Continuity Tracker.)

**10. "Italicized '*It was always finite*'" — converts image to thesis**
A passage uses italics to name the chapter's insight directly. Per style guide, italics are reserved for Ash's own interiority in genuine interior-voice moments, not for thematic declarations. More critically, naming the insight converts what the chapter has been showing through image (edges, floor of a lake, water going down) into a stated thesis. Fix: cut the italicized declaration. The images have already done the work. (Thematic Compass, Voice Editor.)
