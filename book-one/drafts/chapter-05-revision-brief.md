# Revision Brief — Chapter 5: "The Testing"

## Critical Issues (must fix)

### 1. Timeline contradiction: "six weeks" → "three weeks"
**Source:** Continuity Tracker
Chapter 4 establishes Ash has worked for Dorenne for three weeks. Chapter 5 is set three days later but says "six weeks" four times (lines 5, 335, 341, 423). Fix all four instances to "three weeks."

### 2. Floor number: "fourth floor" → "fifth floor"
**Source:** Continuity Tracker
Line 399 references "the fourth floor. The blown-out wall." Chapters 1 and 4 establish the family apartment on the fifth floor. Change to "fifth floor."

### 3. POV breach: examiner's interiority (line 43)
**Source:** Voice Editor
"He'd processed hundreds of these assessments — maybe thousands" presents the examiner's internal experience as fact. Filter through Ash's perception: "He might have processed hundreds..." or cut the sentence.

### 4. POV breach: "She meant:" (line 415)
**Source:** Voice Editor, Arc Guardian, Thematic Compass
"She meant: the Wellspring is confirmed... The asset is verified." Three agents flag this. The declarative "She meant:" reads as omniscient narration. Reframe as Ash's inference. Additionally, "the asset is verified" front-loads Dorenne's eventual betrayal too early — soften to something like "The situation is manageable."

## Important Suggestions (should address)

### 5. Vasra over-described (9 details → 2-3)
**Source:** Voice Editor
Vasra's introduction packs nine physical details across six lines (tall, stooped, white hair, loose knot, long coat, deep pockets, expression, dark sharp eyes, wrinkle description). The style guide calls for two or three. Keep the stoop, the eyes, and the coat.

### 6. Vasra's trickle explanation is exposition Ash already knows
**Source:** World Keeper, Voice Editor
Lines 259-263: Vasra explains how trickles work to a 23-year lifelong resident of Solathis. He would know this. Jump straight to what's different about Ash: "Normal trickles replenish during sleep. Yours didn't establish at birth..."

### 7. "Eighty percent" is a system number, not a character thought
**Source:** World Keeper
Line 373: "The eighty percent of energy bleeding uselessly into the air." A person channeling for three days wouldn't know this figure. Replace with qualitative language: "most of it" or "almost all of it."

### 8. Cut "Remember that" from Vasra's aquifer speech
**Source:** Thematic Compass
The final "Remember that" turns scientific observation into oracular instruction. The reader will remember without being told to. Cut it; the aquifer metaphor is strong enough alone.

### 9. Reduce the Dorenne debrief by ~50% (lines 351-441)
**Source:** Story Architect, Pace Keeper
The section sags — Ash recaps testing the reader already witnessed, reflects on identity shift already covered in the alone-in-the-room scene, and the "vast/oceanic/his" note lands three times instead of once. Keep: document delivery, Dorenne's reaction, her routing Vasra's report, her "proper channels" line. Cut: Ash's recap, repeated identity interiority, the extended city walk.

### 10. Trim the third repetition of "fixed vs. infinite" suppression
**Source:** Pace Keeper, Thematic Compass
The selective-hearing beat appears three times: after Vasra speaks, when Ash withholds from Dorenne, and in closing paragraphs. The closing version (lines 435-441) re-describes the mechanism of suppression rather than enacting it. Either trust the reader after two beats, or let the closing simply not mention the cautionary words — the absence demonstrates the suppression better than describing it.

### 11. Reduce the chorus of awe by one voice
**Source:** Pace Keeper
Four separate characters react with astonishment. The broken equipment speaks for itself. Cut or compress the thin-faced examiner's parting commentary about Vasra (lines 323-327). The scene is stronger when evidence does the work.

### 12. "Null" vs. "dead weight" terminology
**Source:** World Keeper
Line 41 uses "Null" but magic-system.md lists "dead weight" as the in-world term. Either use "dead weight" or add "null" to the vocabulary reference.

### 13. Repetitive "X looked at Y" construction
**Source:** Voice Editor
The "looked at / looked at / looked at" pattern appears three times (lines 77, 205, 359). Keep the first instance (strongest). Vary the others.

## Strengths (what's working — preserve these)

- **The too-small chair** — every reviewer praised this. Physical metaphor for Ash's anomaly. Do not cut.
- **"I can pay for that"** — the most Ash line in the chapter. Comic, endearing, characterologically perfect.
- **The testing montage** — escalating failures with dry humor. The weight embedded in the ceiling is a recurring visual anchor. Best pacing in the chapter.
- **The kettle-prayer closing** — the best single passage. Renders willful blindness through physical metaphor without naming it.
- **"Hands in pockets for three days"** — nine words that tell us everything about Ash's relationship to the new power.
- **The ramp/lift choice** — Ash can use the lift but chooses the ramp. Identity holding vs. identity shifting, rendered through action.
- **Waste heat as ambient metaphor** — the room warming with each test is elegant worldbuilding-through-consequence.
- **Ash's selective hearing** — the prose participates in Ash's denial rather than observing it. The reader who's paying attention sees the warning; the swept-up reader misses it.
- **Dorenne's corridor walk** — breaking her own "dismiss from behind the desk" pattern signals genuine emotion beneath the strategy.
- **"The stone didn't move. It vanished."** — best single-sentence moment.
- **Vasra's aquifer metaphor** — strongest Thread 1 seed. Concrete, vivid, will reward re-reading.
