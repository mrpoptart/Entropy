# Revision Brief — Chapter 17: "The Hero of Solathis"

## Critical Issues (must fix)

**1. Council vote arithmetic — internal contradiction**
The chapter states "The vote was eleven to one. Toraven abstained." An abstention is neither an "eleven to one" nor a vote against. If twelve councillors sit in the chamber and Toraven abstains, the vote is 11-0 with one abstention, not 11-1. Fix: either change the count ("eleven in favor, none opposed, one abstaining") or change Toraven's action from abstention to a dissenting vote. (Continuity Tracker.)

**2. "Months ago" — timeline violation**
Line 119: "That had been months ago. It felt like someone else's life." The book's stated total span is approximately eight weeks. The aide period (pre-Wellspring) was roughly weeks one through three. Fix: "weeks ago." (Continuity Tracker.)

**3. Dalla/Verren naming conflict — still unresolved**
`chapter-11.md` line 81 still reads "Verren, who handled the scheduling." Chapters 15 and 16 use "Dalla." Chapter 17 does not introduce either name, but every chapter written past this point makes the repair harder. Fix before chapter 18 is drafted: update `chapter-11.md` line 81 from "Verren" to "Dalla." (Continuity Tracker — fourth flag; mandatory.)

**4. "He is a person, not a tool" — thesis stated explicitly in dialogue**
Dorenne's correction of Toraven (paragraph 99) names the central tension of the trilogy in direct dialogue: "The Wellspring is Ash. He is a person, not a tool." This is the theme of Books One and Two announced at full volume in the penultimate-act golden chapter, seventeen chapters too early. The irony of Dorenne defending Ash's personhood while allocating him as a resource is real and correct — but it needs to be performed, not declared. Fix: cut "He is a person, not a tool." Replace with a correction performed rather than stated — Dorenne using his name specifically and deliberately where Toraven used "the Wellspring," the argument made by substitution rather than by announcement. (Thematic Compass, Voice Editor.)

**5. "Partnership. The word surfaced and sat in his chest alongside the warmth, and he let it stay." — names the feeling**
Paragraph 131. The preceding corridor exchange has already delivered the partnership beat through action — Dorenne's "I know. That's why it worked," Ash watching her steady stride. The named italicized word reduces what the scene showed. Fix: cut the final sentence ("Partnership. The word surfaced..."). End the section after "he believed it too." The partnership is complete without the announcement. (Thematic Compass — flagged high priority; Arc Guardian flags the same line for different reasons — see Issue 9.)

## Important Suggestions (should address)

**6. "It lived somewhere else. Somewhere older. Somewhere the Wellspring couldn't reach and didn't need to." — over-explains the image**
Paragraph 227. "A warmth in his body that had nothing to do with the reservoir" is complete. The two additional sentences explain the image's meaning instead of trusting the reader to hold it. Fix: cut from "It lived somewhere else" through the end of that sentence. Stop at "a warmth in his body that had nothing to do with the reservoir." (Thematic Compass, Voice Editor.)

**7. Redundant closing declaration after the Ashyn scene**
Paragraph 231: "But it wasn't for the image. It wasn't for the story. It was for the tea, and the baby's name, and the door that was open." This is a restatement of what the two preceding paragraphs already established. Fix: cut the paragraph, or reduce to the list only ("the tea, and the baby's name, and the door that was open") without the negating setup — the list standing alone as the section's close is stronger. (Thematic Compass.)

**8. Opening walk is four beats where two would suffice**
The chapter's opening sequence runs the street sweeper encounter, walking-east celebrity recognition, overheard conversation fragments, and the children's game before the first scene break — approximately 700 words of accumulation doing the same structural work. The children's game is the strongest of the four (it is active, warm, dramatized, emotionally loaded) and should stay. The overheard conversation fragments are the weakest (reported speech, passive observation) and should be cut. Fix: keep the street sweeper image as the opening (compressed: the landmark simile, the smile, his raised hand); keep the children's game in full; cut the middle overheard-conversations section. Target approximately 350 words, half the current length. (Story Architect, Voice Editor.)

**9. "The image she was building" — anticipates Ch18/19 clarity**
Arc Guardian flags one phrase as outside Ch17's timeline: Ash predicting "She might even approve — the hero of Solathis... the story of it better than any deployment for the image she was building." At Ch17, Ash still genuinely believes in the partnership; attributing image-management strategy to Dorenne's thought process belongs to the more suspicious Ash of Ch18-19. Fix: replace with something that registers her scheduling intelligence without attributing strategic image-construction to his perception. E.g., "Dorenne would note the gap. She logged everything. She might even appreciate the story in it, the human texture of a deployment that ran long because someone made tea." (Arc Guardian.)

**10. Reservoir seed is buried under the chapter's emotional resolution — needs repositioning or sharpening**
The chapter ends on warmth: bread still warm, city glowing, earned dreamless sleep. The reservoir observation is the third-to-last section and is explicitly set aside by Ash within it. The reader's final experience of Ch17 is contentment, not unease. This is the seed Ch18 grows from; it should have more charge. Fix option A: move the reservoir observation to the final scene — after the evening work, as the last beat before sleep, so the reader goes to sleep with Ash carrying something he can't examine rather than bread-warmth. Option B: sharpen the push-away so it costs something — "He let it go" reads as comfortable release; the push should feel effortful, a conscious choice to agree with the morning's schedule rather than sit with the perception. (Convergence Tracker.)

**11. Evening work sequence — too long, saps momentum before the reservoir scene**
The water junction, retaining wall, and bridge support scene occupies a full section with no dramatic stakes. The work is clean, efficient, and unremarkable. It exists to show elapsed time and reconstruction continuity, but it deflates the chapter's energy after the tea scene and arrives before the chapter's most structurally important moment (the reservoir observation). Fix: reduce to a single paragraph of summary, or cut entirely. Relocate the efficiency observation ("He wasted less now") into the closing walk home, where it already appears. (Story Architect.)

**12. Haran and Thread 7 — dormancy warning, Ch20 payoff at risk**
Threads 5 and 7 (Efficiency Technique and Haran's Hidden Mastery) have been dormant since Ch13 — four consecutive chapters — and both converge in Ch20. The single oblique reference in line 243 ("Not Haran's micro-channeling precision") keeps Thread 5 technically alive but does nothing for Thread 7 or for either thread's emotional energy. Ch20's "what do you want to build?" must feel like a convergence, not a greeting card from a character the reader has forgotten. Fix: in Ch17's reconstruction scenes, give Haran a brief presence — a physical appearance at a work site (his district is adjacent to reconstruction routes), a memory dramatized rather than observed, or a piece of the workshop that Ash passes. The Ch18 outline note that a neighbor's cracking wall brings Ash to the tinkerer district is a natural opportunity to reactivate Thread 7 one chapter before its payoff. Either path: one of Ch17 or Ch18 must bring Haran back into active relationship before Ch20. (Convergence Tracker.)

**13. Levelers thread — needs a second anchor point before its 7-chapter dormancy**
Thread 3's Ch22 payoff requires Ash to read a broadsheet and recognize his story in theirs — a re-reader recognition moment. The thread's only anchor is the Ch15 gorge-rim confrontation (embedded in crisis conditions). Seven chapters of dormancy from that single vivid moment risks the Ch22 recognition landing as intellectual rather than emotional. Fix: in Ch17's city-walk section, one detail — a broadsheet on a wall that Ash walks past without reading, a conversation fragment about redistribution overheard and not processed, the contrast between intact elder heights and rebuilding lower districts observed with a specific political framing — would give Thread 3 a second anchor point before its long silence. Ash doesn't need to engage it; noticing-and-not-engaging is exactly right for his arc position. (Convergence Tracker.)

**14. Efficiency improvement told, not shown**
The narration states "He wasted less now... The reservoir's force applied with an efficiency that would have astonished him a month ago" (line 243). The water junction scene, which is the natural home for demonstrating this, renders the work in outcome terms only. Fix: add two or three sentences in the water junction section where Ash notices the work feeling different — the energy following a specific path, a fracture closing with less force than he expected, a moment of surprise at his own precision. Show the comparison rather than stating it. (Pace Keeper.)

**15. Transit shaft repair is entirely offscreen**
The emotionally resonant consequence scene (workers watching the first supply loads arrive, people gathering at the shaft because it "meant something about the world being put back together") is fully dramatized; the repair producing it is one dependent clause. Fix: a brief paragraph of present-tense experience before the consequence scene — Ash at the shaft, hands on stone, the work itself — grounds the emotional payoff in physical reality. Does not need to be long; needs to be present. (Pace Keeper.)

**16. Dorenne's 40-minute presentation is mostly summarized**
Ash concludes "Partnership" and "magnificent" after watching a presentation of which the reader sees two quoted passages. The seduction must be felt by the reader, not taken on faith. Fix: add one dramatized moment from within the presentation — Toraven challenges a premise, Dorenne's response shows the precise quality of mind Ash admires — so that his "magnificent" evaluation has something specific to attach to. One exchange is sufficient. (Pace Keeper.)

**17. Add brief Ryn interiority beat to maintain thread warmth**
Ryn is absent from Ch16 and Ch17. Her absence is structurally correct but two chapters of silence risks the thread cooling. Fix: one brief beat where her name or the walkway conversation surfaces and gets submerged — two sentences, quickly suppressed. The pattern established in Ch14 (her face appearing at the edge of his mind while he's thinking about something else) should recur at least once through the earthquake aftermath. (Arc Guardian.)

**18. Add brief Maren interiority beat**
Maren's swollen hands under the table were loaded with significance in Ch14. Two chapters of complete silence risks losing the accumulation. Fix: a single passing thought, quickly submerged — consistent with Ash's pattern of seeing and not lifting. (Arc Guardian.)
