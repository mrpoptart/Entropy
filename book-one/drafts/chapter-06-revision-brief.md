## Critical Fixes (must do)

### 1. Replace the scaling stone demonstration with the lattice demonstration
**Source:** Editor's Table (Lyricist pull)
The Carver uses a scaling stone — an abstract display tool. The Lyricist uses the Hall's actual heating lattice, which is narratively stronger: it demonstrates practical utility, connects to Thread 2 (Infrastructure Decline), and gives the audience a visceral physical experience (the room warming from cold stone to summer) rather than just a visual one (a stone glowing). Adapt the Lyricist's lattice approach: Ash places his palm on the wall, activates channels that required six elder-generation channelers working in rotation, and the room goes from cold to warm in a single sustained pulse. The waste-heat detail can remain but should be the room's *intended* warming, not just bleed-off. The audience feels it in their skin. The channeling strips become redundant. The Councillor's assessment becomes more concrete — she's calculating what a heated chamber means multiplied across a city.

Cut or compress:

### 3. Reduce X-as-Y constructions to 2-3 per chapter
**Source:** Editor's Table
Currently overused: "the architecture of a smile," "the grammar of management," "the syntax of narrative," "the acoustic equivalent of attention." Keep the two strongest (recommend "the architecture of a smile" and "the acoustic equivalent of attention"). Cut or rewrite the others into more direct observations.
