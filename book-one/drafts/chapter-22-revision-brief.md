# Revision Brief — Chapter 22: "The Walk Home"

## Critical Issues (must fix)

**1. "kilojoules" / "kJ" used as in-world notation — four instances**
Lines 21, 99, 133, 229: Ash speaks or thinks in kJ notation. `magic-system.md` is explicit: kilojoules are the author's measurement system; in-world characters and narration do not use this term. This is the project's most persistent worldbuilding violation, flagged across chapters 14–22. Fix:
- Line 21: "Every drop of it" or simply "All of it."
- Line 99: "a daily budget and watched it buy less every year" — "budget" is already established in-world vocabulary
- Line 133: "the absolute necessity of making every drop count"
- Line 229: "Twenty-two million, give or take. A season. Maybe two." (The number alone carries the weight; the unit is author-brain leaking through.)
(World Keeper — critical; Voice Editor concurs.)

**2. Maren's psychology named too precisely from outside — oversteps what Ash can know**
Lines 163–166: "The discipline. The guilt. The excellence that wasn't ambition but apology — the relentless, grinding overperformance of a person who'd been born with a pool her brother didn't get and had spent every day since trying to be worthy of the difference." This is Ash performing precise psychological analysis of his sister's interior life — her guilt, her compensatory excellence, the architecture of her apology — which he cannot access from a street corner at 1 a.m. watching her work in a trench. The Maren profile says she has "never named or examined" this guilt. If Ash can diagnose it with clinical precision, the eventual scene (Books Two or Three) where she names it herself is pre-empted.

Fix: Strip back to felt experience. He sees the wince, the jaw, the shoulders, the controlled expenditure, the move to the next crack without rest. He feels something hot and imprecise — not a named theory, but the recognition that something has always been there and he hasn't been looking. "She'd been carrying this her whole life" can stay. "The guilt. The excellence that wasn't ambition but apology..." should go or be reduced to a half-formed intuition: "he thought he recognized it — something like guilt underneath the discipline, something he didn't yet have a word for." Let him understand that she's been carrying something; do not let him understand what. (Arc Guardian — critical; Pace Keeper, Thematic Compass concur.)

**3. POV violation — Dorenne's desk after Ash has left**
The chapter contains a closing image of Dorenne's desk — or a scene-interior detail attributed to Dorenne — after Ash has left the building. Third-person limited means Ash's physical position constrains what narration can access. Additionally, if this echoes the "name begun and crossed out" image from Ch21's closing paragraph (which was given to the reader as the author's grace note, outside Ash's POV), importing it into Ash's street-level imagination blurs the POV boundary and weakens both moments. Fix: keep everything Ash can plausibly observe or infer from outside. The specific image of the crossed-out name should be reframed as "maybe" or "he would never know" — uncertainty is truer to his situation than vision. (Voice Editor — critical POV violation; Pace Keeper concurs.)

**4. Broadsheet connection over-explained — three sentences where one would do**
Lines 99–101: "He'd been living their lives at high speed. The Wellspring's decline was the world's decline, compressed. He was a miniature of the crisis — one man, one reservoir, one countdown — and the shape of it was the same shape, and the panic was the same panic, and the question at the end was the same question: *What do you build with what's left?*" The broadsheet scene earns the parallel through action (he reads it twice, touches the paper). The paragraph diagrams what the reader has already felt. Additionally, the italicized question here undermines the second, correct instance of the same question in the workshop — when it returns there as Haran's voice, it must feel like an arrival, not a reprise. Fix: cut to one line — something as spare as "The same math. The same countdown." Remove the italicized question from this location; protect it at the workshop. (Thematic Compass — critical; Voice Editor, Pace Keeper concur.)

**5. Reservoir figure needs verification against chapter 21**
Line 229: "Twenty-two million kilojoules, give or take." The outline's reservoir table sets Book One ending at approximately 50% (~25M), but the chapter's figure of 22M represents ~44%. Chapter 22 explicitly shows Ash not channeling, so no depletion occurs during this chapter. Either: (a) chapter 21 established 22M explicitly and the outline needs updating, or (b) the chapter 22 figure should be ~25M to match the outline's projection. Check chapter 21's specific number and align. (World Keeper — critical arithmetic consistency issue; Continuity Tracker concurs.)

## Important Suggestions (should address)

**6. Chapter is over-length for its position — three specific cuts**
At approximately 3,900 words, the chapter runs above the convergence zone target and above what a closing chapter needs. Three targeted cuts would bring it within range without touching the load-bearing material:

- **Thornwall walk: trim by ~300 words.** The thesis lands at "the knowledge changes the view" (line 13). Everything after illustrates rather than develops. The anonymous-gift-in-the-stone idea is stated twice in different registers. Keep the walk, the first emotional acknowledgment ("he'd do it again"), the tight throat. Cut the repetition. The Dorenne window arrives faster and hits harder.
- **Infrastructure observation section: cut or radically compress (~300 words).** This section has no scene function at Chapter 22. The "fingers in a dam" image can be transplanted into the Thornwall opening as a single sentence; the rest is information the reader already holds from Ch19. Cutting it removes a comfortable stopping place and eliminates the only section that reads as expendable.
- **Workshop section second half: trim by ~40% from "The reservoir sat in his chest" onward.** The first half of the workshop (entering the dark, the smell, the refusal to channel, sitting at the bench) earns its space. The second half circles the same thematic ground three or four times. Everything from "The reservoir sat in his chest, warm and finite" to the first filing beat can be compressed to a single paragraph. The final three paragraphs (Maren asleep somewhere, Velden pump, extended return of Haran's question) should be reduced from ~400 words to ~150.
(Story Architect — highest structural priority.)

**7. Dorenne window scene — Ash's certainty about her inner state**
"She was recalculating. He knew this the way he knew the weight of his own reservoir. She was recalculating the deployment schedule, adjusting the sequence of remaining sites, optimizing the allocation..." — this is narrated as confirmed fact, but Ash is on the street below at night. The first inference ("she would be recalculating") is plausible from months of close observation. The specific operational detail that follows goes beyond what street-level inference supports. Fix: shift from assertion to inference — "She would be recalculating" or "He could imagine her at it — the charts, the pencil, the sequence shifting." The uncertainty is truer to Ash's situation and more emotionally resonant. (Voice Editor; Pace Keeper concurs.)

**8. "This was what finitude looked like when you didn't have the luxury of ignoring it" — captions the image**
Line 153. This sentence arrives after one of the chapter's most effective stretches of observational writing. Maren kneeling at the next crack immediately after finishing the first is the statement. This sentence is the caption under the photograph. Cut it. End the beat on the physical. (Thematic Compass; Voice Editor concurs.)

**9. Narrator over-explains after "I am not a water main" / equivalent confrontation line**
If this chapter contains any echo of Ch21's break in narration, the explanatory paragraph following the chapter's strongest line should be cut. The line does the work. (Voice Editor — flagged for any such passage here.)

**10. Broadsheet-to-Maren transition: remove scene break to create collision**
Thread 3 (Levelers) and Thread 8 (Maren) are designated by the convergence map to amplify each other in Ch22. The draft puts them in adjacent sections rather than continuous motion. If the scene break between the broadsheet section and the Maren section is removed, Ash moves continuously from reading the broadsheet to rounding the corner and seeing Maren — the two arithmetics (the young channelers' decline and his sister's body) arrive as a single recognition rather than two separate realizations. The infrastructure passage becomes transition rather than section. (Convergence Tracker.)

**11. "She was" repetition string — runs one or two beats long**
Lines 39–42: seven sentences beginning with "She was" or "she would" in close succession. The anaphora is intentional and effective — it mimics Ash's circling knowledge of her habits — but it runs past its peak. Break the chain at "She was doing her job" and let the next sentence shift construction: "Brilliantly. With the precision and competence of thirty years." (Voice Editor.)

**12. Ryn — one light forward gesture**
Ryn is absent from Ch22, correctly. But a half-beat interior mention during the walk (her name surfacing and not landing) would keep the thread alive as an unresolved question Ash carries out of Book One. The convergence map does not require her in this chapter; one sentence of passing thought is the correct level of presence — not a scene, not a resolution, just: she exists in the inventory of what isn't settled. (Arc Guardian.)

**13. Thread 9 (Mother) — consider one acknowledgment beat**
The mother has been dormant since Ch18, and the convergence map does not assign her to Ch22. But the chapter walks Ash through the city as an emotional map of his relationships, and the building his family lived in is never in his path. One passing thought — even "not toward his parents' old building" with a half-beat of feeling — would close a thread the convergence map otherwise leaves with no Book One gesture of completion. Low priority; the convergence map's designation takes precedence if this doesn't fit naturally. (Continuity Tracker; Arc Guardian flags as optional.)

**14. The pump paragraph — "because no one else was going to" tips into editorializing**
Line 258: the closing clause "because no one else was going to" frames the pump's users as abandoned rather than self-determining, which tips the prose toward authorial commentary. The pump passage is already strong. Cut the final clause; end at "water would flow." (Thematic Compass.)

**15. The workshop closing — end on the external rather than the internal**
The penultimate passage ("In the Velden building, the pump sat in its housing — silent, ready, the spring-loaded mechanism waiting for morning hands") is the chapter's best forward-facing image and arrives one paragraph before the end. "His hands remembered" is the correct internal closing note, but if the chapter ends there it tapers into interiority. Consider ending on the external — the pump waiting, three families at dawn, water flowing without magic — with "His hands remembered" as the second-to-last beat that earns it. This makes Book One close on the world that's about to be built, not the man not-yet-knowing how to build it. (Convergence Tracker; Story Architect concurs.)

## Strengths (what's working — preserve)

- **The opening line and first paragraph.** "The Directorate doors closed behind him and the night was there, wide and cold." The warmth leaving the stone, "a held breath releasing." The entire Book One arc is in this image. Do not revise the first paragraph.
- **"He was part of the stone now."** Anonymous labor as legacy. The sacrifice-as-choice theme at its most resonant, delivered through physical metaphor. Keep exactly.
- **The Dorenne window scene.** The steadily-burning lamp, the imagined pencil, "the last filament of the hope that she would be different from what she was." The line "Not because she was small. Because the framework was" is the cleanest possible encapsulation of Dorenne. Protect against any impulse to explain or soften it. He turns south; he doesn't look back. Do not touch the core of this scene.
- **"He had loved being seen by her."** The emotional center of the entire Dorenne arc. Protect.
- **The broadsheet scene's physical detail.** Ash reading it twice, touching the edge of the paper, the hand-press printing — "hands and sweat, no pool required." A textbook example of thematic content delivered through tactile experience. Protect.
- **The celebration dinner callback.** Maren's news "settling to the bottom of the evening like sediment in a glass." Specific, terrible, and right. This is new material not in the outline and better than the outline. Protect.
- **The Maren scene as a whole.** The best single scene in the book's convergence zone. The pool flickering, the wince in her hands, the jaw tightening, the move to the next crack without rest. The decision not to call out — and what Ash commits to instead ("he would talk to her. Not tonight") — is exactly right. Do not shorten, do not make it more elegant, do not resolve differently.
- **"Not because she was small. Because the framework was."** The chapter's sharpest line. Do not elaborate.
- **Not channeling in the workshop.** "The impulse was there — faint, automatic... and he let it pass." Behavioral evidence of who Ash is becoming, expressed as restraint rather than declaration. The choice not to do something. Protect.
- **The layered-stone geological passage** (elder channeling at the core, thinning generation by generation). Thread 2 (Infrastructure) at its most effective in the book.
- **"The work was always real."** Four words. Do not elaborate.
- **The dawn without channeling.** "The world turned toward light the way water turned toward downhill, inevitably, without anyone channeling it there." The trilogy's destination in a single sentence. Protect.
- **The final line.** "His hands remembered." Correct landing. All cuts should preserve a path to this.
- **The flagstone callback.** Ash stepping over the flagstone that always tipped, without thinking. One of the book's best continuity details — invisible to a first-time reader, earned resonance on re-read.
- **The Velden building pump.** "Three families would come at dawn and turn the crank, twenty minutes each... water would flow. No pool. No reservoir." Thread 14 (Stewardship) at its most concrete. Keep exactly.
