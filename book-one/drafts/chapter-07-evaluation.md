## Criterion-by-Criterion Analysis

### 1. Voice & Style

**Carver (9/10).** The strongest voice match to chapters 4-5. Clean, declarative prose that trusts the reader completely. Narrative distance is medium baseline throughout, pulling to deep close only at the right moments. At approximately 5,500 words, the Carver is under the 8,000-word target.

**Hearthkeeper (8/10).** Warmer prose, more expansive description. The mother's arrival scene is the best-written single scene across all three drafts. However, the draft over-explains in places. At approximately 7,700 words, it's close to target.

**Lyricist (7/10).** The most overtly thematic prose. Strong rhythmic control in individual passages, but two significant style guide violations: (1) enters the father's perspective, breaking third-person-limited POV. (2) The narrator confesses that the preceding paragraphs were NOT Ash's thoughts, breaking the style guide's rule that "when Ash is in denial, the prose lies too." At approximately 6,800 words.

### 2. Scene Execution

**Carver (8/10).** All five brief beats are covered efficiently. The packing scene is the least dramatized — reads more as narrated summary than lived scene. The breaking/repair sequence covers four incidents without repeating the same dramatic shape.

**Hearthkeeper (9/10).** The strongest scene execution. Every beat is fully dramatized in real time. The mother's arrival at the new apartment is a complete scene with dialogue, physicality, and emotional undercurrent. The maintenance worker with her card is a smart externalizing detail.

**Lyricist (8/10).** Strong opening — the father's chair on the level floor is the best single detail for the new-apartment scene. The packing scene is the most emotionally charged but the mother's physical capability strains credibility given Ch4's injury.

### 3. Emotional Precision

**Carver (9/10).** The emotional arc — "Ash sees only improvement. The reader sees what's being lost" — is executed with near-perfect precision. The gap between Ash's perspective and the reader's is maintained throughout.

**Hearthkeeper (8/10).** Strong emotional execution. The "I know how a stove works, Ashlyn" exchange is the single most emotionally efficient moment across all drafts. However, the father's unasked question is italicized, naming what should remain unnameable.

**Lyricist (7/10).** The most emotionally explicit, which is both strength and weakness. Repeatedly explains the emotional subtext to the reader rather than letting it land.

### 4. Character Authenticity

**Carver (9/10).** All four key characters behave consistently with their profiles. Maren is perfect — she fixes things without announcement. The coat-on-coat-off sequence is the most character-authentic Maren moment in any draft.

**Hearthkeeper (8/10).** Strong character work, particularly the parents. However, the italicized unasked question is a characterization shortcut.

**Lyricist (7/10).** Strongest individual father moments but worst POV compliance. Maren's "Then stop touching them until you can control it" is a direct confrontation, which the brief explicitly prohibits.

### 5. Thread Delivery

**Carver (9/10).** All four threads progressed exactly as specified.

**Hearthkeeper (9/10).** All four threads progressed. The maintenance worker's card is a unique Thread 8 detail.

**Lyricist (8/10).** Threads progressed but with compliance issues. Thread 8 weakened by Maren's confrontational dialogue.

### 6. Worldbuilding Integration

All three drafts score **8/10** with different strengths. Carver: functional detail. Hearthkeeper: lived-in texture. Lyricist: economic contrast.

### 7. Continuity

**Carver (9/10).** Consistent with chapters 4-5. Door wedge callback connects to Ch4's closing image.

**Hearthkeeper (8/10).** Good continuity with one possible error — uses "Torren" as a given name when Ch5 established it as the family surname.

**Lyricist (7/10).** Mother has a splint rather than cane, timeline potentially inconsistent. Missing door wedge callback.

## Best Passages

### Carver
- **"The look of a woman sitting in a room where everything worked and nothing was hers."** The single best line across all three drafts.
- **The coat sequence:** Maren takes off her coat, repairs the lighting strip for twenty minutes, puts her coat back on, leaves for work late. Five sentences. Everything about Maren's character rendered through physical actions.
- **The closing:** "She slept in the next room, in a bed she hadn't chosen, in a building she hadn't warmed, in a neighborhood where nobody knew the sound of her footsteps on the stairs... The silence was the kind that nobody had made."

### Hearthkeeper
- **"I know how a stove works, Ashlyn."** Four words plus the full name. The most emotionally efficient line in any draft.
- **The father at the corridor wall:** "His palm flat against the stone the way Ash's mother laid her palm against the heating lattice — touching, measuring, learning a surface that wasn't yet familiar."
- **The mother's first tea** from her own kettle, propped against the counter with her cane.

### Lyricist
- **The chair on the level floor:** "The chair sat flat and still and obedient, and his father's face did something complicated — surprise and something adjacent to loss, the expression of a man who had spent years shimming furniture against uneven floors."
- **"She looked at the light on the stone floor the way you look at something you're trying to memorize."**
- **The father's bag:** "His hand rested on it for a moment — not adjusting the weight, not testing the balance. Just resting."

## Recommended Path

### Primary Recommendation: Build from the Carver

The Carver should serve as the foundation draft. It has the strongest POV discipline, the most precise emotional calibration, and the most devastating closing passage. Its restraint is the chapter's greatest asset.

### Passages to Transplant

1. **From the Hearthkeeper: the mother's arrival scene.** The "I know how a stove works, Ashlyn" exchange and the first tea from the old kettle. Compress to ~500 words.
2. **From the Hearthkeeper: the father at the corridor wall.** Insert into the neighborhood section.
3. **From the Lyricist: the chair on the level floor.** Insert into the early moving-in section.
4. **From the Hearthkeeper: the maintenance worker's card.** Adds institutional texture to the breaking/repair sequence.
5. **From the Lyricist: the mother looking at light** — "the way you look at something you're trying to memorize."

### What Not to Pull

- The Hearthkeeper's two-kettle structure — one kettle is the right image
- The Lyricist's father-perspective passage — POV violation
- Either draft's italicized father question — must stay in gesture
- The Lyricist's Maren confrontation — she must not confront
- The Hearthkeeper's second gift (the bowl) — extends without adding dimension

### Revision Notes

1. **Expand the packing scene** by ~300-400 words, dramatizing 2-3 objects in real time
2. **Add the mother's arrival** from the Hearthkeeper (~500 words)
3. **Strengthen one neighborhood interaction** with 4-6 lines of actual dialogue
4. **Keep the Carver's closing exactly as written** — it's the chapter's strongest pages
5. **Keep Maren silent** — her silence is the point

---

## Overall Recommendation

**Build from the Carver.** Its POV discipline, emotional precision, and stylistic restraint make it the clear foundation. The closing passage is the best sustained writing in the project to date.

The Hearthkeeper contributes the best individual scene (the mother's arrival) and the best father detail (the corridor wall). The Lyricist contributes the best opening detail (the chair on the level floor) and the best mother-observation line ("trying to memorize"). Both should be drawn on selectively.

The Lyricist's draft, despite containing individually beautiful passages, has structural problems that disqualify it as a foundation: POV violations, a Maren characterization that contradicts the brief, and a narrator that explains rather than shows.

The Carver gets the architecture right. The reader sees what Ash misses. The mother keeps the old kettle. Nobody discusses it. The silence is the kind that nobody made. This is the chapter.
