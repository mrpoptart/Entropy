## Important Suggestions (should address)

**3. Ridgewall self-analysis passage is too articulate for Ash's current arc position**
The "twenty-three years of nothing" passage names Ash's pre-power grief with a precision that his profile says should live below language at this stage: "Twenty-three years of the cheerful performance, the careful smile, the private math of justifying his presence by being pleasant enough to offset the cost of his existence." At this point in his arc he should be too intoxicated by the new identity to excavate the old one this clearly. The emotional truth is correct; the self-articulation is slightly advanced. Fix: condense toward sensation and action — the building warming, the reservoir through his palms, the woman at the door — and let the thematic weight carry without Ash fully naming what he's escaping.

**4. Two identity passages repeat the same point — consolidate**
The Ridgewall scene (twenty-three years, "I was an instrument that fit the world's need," the woman with the child) and the closing meditation ("He was the one whose hands could hold what others couldn't... he needed to be needed the way the heating lattice needed power") make the same central argument twice. The Ridgewall version is more vivid and physical — let it do the full work. In the closing meditation, cut the four sentences beginning "He was one of them now" through "The one who went where the need was." Let the closing meditation focus on what it alone can do: the enumeration of things he doesn't think about (Ryn, Haran, his mother's tea, the broadsheet) while only thinking about what he can do.

**5. Two Leveler seeding passes in quick succession**
The Council plaza (banners, woman's appraising gaze, broadsheets) and the community hall (door open, three voice fragments about dependency) accomplish the same structural task. The community hall version contains the most precise seed: "dependent on one person. What happens when he's not available?" But having both in quick succession slightly dilutes each. Fix: keep both locations, but trim the community hall to one voice fragment rather than three. The "dependent on one person" line should survive; the others can go.

**6. "He was thinking in systems now" — explicit statement before a showing scene**
The sentence "He was thinking in systems now. Dorenne's systems, mapped onto the city's bones, each deployment a node in a network of maintenance that kept the city standing" arrives immediately before the deployment notes scene, which shows exactly this. The statement pre-empts what the scene demonstrates. Fix: delete the sentence. Trust the notes scene — the junction order, estimated flow rates, particular characteristics of each interface stone — to carry the meaning.

**7. Four-district deployment montage has no failed or partial moment**
The montage presents four successful deployments without a single waste, frustration, or recalibration that earns the efficiency improvement Verant is tracking. Ash's improvement is claimed but not earned within this chapter. Fix: Within the northern quarter retaining wall scene (already the most dramatized), add one moment where his initial approach is too blunt — Sorren logs a spike in readings, he recalibrates, achieves stabilization on the second pass. The scene already has Sorren as witness; this gives her witnessing something to mean.

**8. Ryn's fade is passive — needs one active beat of not-choosing**
After the second cancellation, "a week passed" and Ryn's silence arrives in narration. The mechanism is summarized rather than dramatized. The actual decision — the one that ends the relationship — happens in a gap between paragraphs. Fix: After the second cancellation, add a short passage (one paragraph) where Ash is at his desk with the deployment brief in one hand and the disc within reach. He knows he should schedule something. He picks up the brief. He reads. The disc stays on the table. No narration explaining this was a mistake — the scene carries it. This converts passive drift into the first small active choice to let something go.

**9. Workshop walk-past may be preempting Chapter 13's planned workshop visit**
The chapter's walk-past scene — Ash sees his workbench, his tools exactly as he left them, Haran at the far bench with his back turned, and keeps walking — is emotionally more complete than a "near miss." If Chapter 13 still intends a workshop visit where Ash enters and tries to work (and strips a fitting's threads), that scene needs clearly different weight. Either pare back the Ch12 walk-past slightly so Ch13 feels new, or verify the Ch13 scene doesn't become redundant.

**10. "He didn't think about any of it" — cut**
In the closing passage: "Ryn's silence sat on the resonance disc. Haran's patience sat in a workshop across the city. His mother's tea sat in a cup she'd poured and not drunk. He didn't think about any of it. He thought about what he could do." — the line "He didn't think about any of it" names the avoidance directly after the chapter has spent pages enacting it. Fix: cut the line. "He thought about what he could do" follows directly from the list and lands harder without the explanatory connector.

**11. Father's nod "carries something — a weight, a direction" — vague**
After the pump conversation, "the nod carried something — a weight, a direction — but Ash was already thinking about the retaining walls" does work it hasn't earned. The chapter has already delivered its best father material: "He talks about them the way people talk about someone who's gone." Fix: end the pump exchange on "Some of it" full stop, or replace the nod with a more specific physical detail. Cut "a weight, a direction" — the gesture should be concrete or absent.

**12. Old kettle motif needs physical grounding**
The chapter shows the mother using the old kettle and mentions the new cups in the back, but doesn't complete the visual beat: the copper kettle Ash bought sitting unused in the cabinet. The convergence map schedules this motif to recur at Ch18; the more weight it carries here, the harder Ch18 lands. Fix: one additional phrase noting the copper kettle's location — unused, cabinet, background — so the image of her pouring from the dented old one compounds against its presence without being named.

**13. Resonance discs not established in prior chapters**
The resonance disc communication technology is introduced here ("paired communication stones that Dorenne's office used for scheduling") without prior establishment. Fix: verify against worldbuilding.md. If not yet documented, add it there. Not a blocking error but a documentation gap — subsequent chapters will use this technology and it should have a canonical source.

**14. Haran/father social connection implied without establishment**
The father says "I saw [the pump] last week — Haran walked me through." This implies Ash's father and Haran are on visiting terms — a relationship not established in chapters 1–11. This is plausible but unearned. Fix: either a brief grounding line here ("they'd met during the early workshop days, when Ash was thirteen") or a planted detail in an earlier chapter. If the connection is intentional, it should be established rather than assumed.

**15. Give Ash one beat of conscious avoidance with the political material**
Ash walks past the banners and broadsheets with no trace of his analytical mind engaging. His profile specifies that he is capable of "willful blindness when hope is on the line" — which is more powerful than literal inability to perceive. He already notices the broadsheet designs as "similar to Haran's." Fix: one moment where his analytical mind identifies what he's seeing (the protest is about him, functionally) and he consciously moves past it. "He recognized the argument and kept walking" — active avoidance rather than passive non-engagement.
