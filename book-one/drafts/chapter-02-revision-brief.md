# Revision Brief — Chapter 2

## Overview

**Current state:** Chapter covers Ash's first day at the infrastructure office. He accomplishes too much too fast — memorizes all team leads, solves a six-month scheduling conflict, defuses a political confrontation, gets promoted to the briefing table, and overhears critical fault data, all on Day One.

**Revision goal:** Expand the timeline to ~two weeks. Show failures before successes. Dramatize the friction of being magicless. Let competence accumulate through observable experience, not summary.

**Target length:** ~12,000–16,000 words (current: ~8,000). If it exceeds 12,000, consider splitting into two chapters at the Week 1/Week 2 boundary.

**Voice selection for revised drafts:** Hearthkeeper + Carver. Hearthkeeper for the warmth and relational texture this chapter needs (Tessaly's silent kindnesses, the Ryn scene, the daily human detail of struggling). Carver for the restraint and physical precision (the channeled-lock humiliations, the briefing-room silence, the economy of Dorenne's approval).

## Scene-by-Scene Revision Map

### Scene 1: Arrival Before Dawn (Lines 1–18)
**Disposition: REVISE**
- **Keep:** Ash arriving early, climbing the ramp, entering the dark office, finding his desk, tidying the central table. This is strong character work.
- **Change:** Slow the opening down. Let him sit at the empty desk longer. The tidying should feel like a displacement activity — he doesn't know what to do, so he does what he knows. Remove the line about the stairs feeling "charged." Day One should feel uncertain, not triumphant.
- **Add:** A beat where he looks at the map board and understands almost nothing. The colored overlays are beautiful and opaque. He can see the pattern but can't read the legend.

### Scene 2: Tessaly's Arrival and Orientation (Lines 22–56)
**Disposition: REVISE**
- **Keep:** Tessaly's sharp assessment, the "New aide" classification, the resonance-stone problem.
- **Change:** Tessaly should be colder — indifferent, not sizing him up with interest. She's had multiple aides cycle through. Her orientation is fast because she's done it too many times, not because she respects his ability to keep up. When she hits the resonance-stone gap, her tone should carry a pragmatic weariness: "That's going to slow us down."
- **Cut:** "He memorized it on the first pass." Replace with him writing the list down and studying it at home that night. Maybe misremembering a name the next day.

### Scene 3: The Morning Unfolds (Lines 59–61)
**Disposition: REPLACE**
- **Current:** Summary paragraph covering the entire morning in one block ("He carried documents between floors... He learned to knock... He learned which doors were latched by channeled locks..."). This is pure tell.
- **Replace with dramatized scenes:**

**NEW Scene 3a: The First Errand — Channeled Lock Failure.**
Tessaly sends him to collect Laris's report on the third level. He goes. Laris's door has a channeled lock. No knocker — why would there be? Everyone announces via resonance stone. Ash stands in the corridor. He waits. He considers knocking on the wall. He waits longer. Eventually someone exits and he catches the door. Laris isn't ready. Barely registers him. Ash returns to Tessaly empty-handed. She doesn't comment. She gives him the next task.

**NEW Scene 3b: The Morning Brief — Silent, Confused Observer.**
Ash sits in the corner. Takes notes. Understands almost nothing of the technical language — "channeling fracture at a junction point" and "pressure tolerances" and "kilojoule budgets" are opaque. He *can* follow the emotional dynamics: who's scared, who's performing confidence, who defers to whom. When Dorenne asks his thoughts afterward, he offers something honest and **wrong** — a misreading based on good observation but wrong vocabulary. Dorenne's response is neutral, not encouraging. Something like "Keep listening" or simply a nod and turn away. Not a test passed — a test deferred.

### Scene 4: The Scheduling Conflict Solution (Lines 95–128)
**Disposition: REPLACE — move to end of Week One**
- **Current:** After two hours of reading aqueduct schedules, Ash solves a six-month conflict that three senior staff couldn't crack. The scene reads as genius arriving fully formed.
- **Replace:** This scene should happen on Day 5 or later. The insight should arrive as **accumulated pattern recognition**, not a flash:
  - By this point, Ash has been walking between Thornwall and Bridgeway offices for days. He's carried scheduling documents back and forth. He walked past the maintenance notice about junction nine three separate times. He didn't set out to solve anything — the pattern assembled itself from proximity.
  - The write-up should feel tentative — he's not sure about the pressure tolerances. He leaves the note under Tessaly's ledger as a "this might be something."
  - **Tessaly's reaction should be understated.** Not "three senior staff couldn't sort it." Instead: she reads the note. Glances at him. Says "Right." Walks away. Later, future scheduling questions start appearing on his desk. The recognition is behavioral, not spoken.

### Scene 5: The Aldren Confrontation (Lines 131–178)
**Disposition: REPLACE — move to Week Two (Day 8-9)**
- **Current:** On Day One, Ash serves tea and drops a correct inference about rotation schedules, deflecting a Council member's anger. Dorenne calls it "political instinct, not hospitality" and promotes him to the briefing table.
- **Replace:** This scene should land in Week Two, after Ash has sat through multiple briefings and watched Dorenne manage several political visitors. The tea move should feel like something he **learned from watching her** — she changes room temperatures with silence, questions, seating arrangements. Tea is his version: lower-status, more human, using the only tool he has (hands, hospitality).
  - The "southern pool rotation" inference should be offered with visible uncertainty. He's guessing. He says so. Dorenne uses it to de-escalate anyway. Later, she tells him it turned out to be correct, and his reaction should be **relief masking surprise** — not quiet confidence. He got lucky. He knows it.
  - Dorenne's "political instinct, not hospitality" speech becomes a quieter acknowledgment. Maybe just: "Well handled." The briefing-table invitation should come separately, at the end of Week One, based on accumulated reliability, not a single triumph.

### NEW Section: Days Two Through Four — "The Mechanics of Showing Up"
**Disposition: NEW — insert between revised Scene 2 and the scheduling conflict**

This is where the chapter can compress time, but around **specific dramatized moments**:

**NEW Scene: Learning Osten (Day 2 → Day 4).**
First visit to the records room: Osten talks for twenty minutes about filing provenance. Ash nods, can't get a word in, leaves without the document. Second visit (Day 3 or 4): Ash tries asking about the filing system's history — flattery disguised as curiosity. It works. Osten produces the document in under a minute.

**NEW Scene: The Lock Problem (ongoing).**
Ash encounters channeled locks again and again. Each time — a small humiliation. Waiting for someone. Asking a passing staffer. Being seen standing helpless. By Day 3, he starts mapping which doors have manual handles. He draws a route through the building that avoids the problem ones. This is clever and sad. The map is a metaphor for everything he does: working around a world not built for him.

**NEW Scene: A Real Mistake (Day 3 or 4).**
Ash delivers a document to the wrong team or misfiles a schedule. Small mistake, but it causes a real problem — a maintenance crew shows up at the wrong junction. Tessaly has to fix it. The office knows. Ash's shame is disproportionate — he feels the error as confirmation that he doesn't belong. Dorenne's response: she doesn't mention it. She gives him the next task as if nothing happened. The restraint says more than encouragement would.

**NEW Scene: Physical Toll (Day 3-4).**
Running up and down the ramp all day carrying documents that could be sent by resonance stone. By Day 3, his legs are cramping. He hides it. One afternoon Tessaly sees him wincing on the ramp. Without a word, she starts grouping his deliveries by floor — routing them so he makes fewer trips. She does this as a scheduling optimization. Ash recognizes it as a kindness because efficiency is Tessaly's language for kindness.

**NEW Scene: Counting Coins (Day 2 or 3).**
Lunchtime. Ash stands in the corridor with food vendors and counts coins carefully. He buys the cheapest option. On Day 3, he skips lunch entirely. This sets up the un-calculated purchase with Ryn.

### Scene 6: Kharren Fault Overhearing (Lines 181–206)
**Disposition: REVISE — move to Week Two**
- **Keep:** The two engineers, the overheard conversation about deep anchors degrading. The content is strong.
- **Change:** Since the timeline is expanded, this lands during Week Two when Ash has enough context to understand *slightly* more of the engineering language (he's been studying vocab at night). The fragments he catches should hit harder because he now has enough framework to feel the fear, even though he still can't fully articulate it.
- **Cut:** "He filed it away. He'd think about it later" — this feels too controlled for a moment that should unsettle him. Let it sit in his body, not his file system.

### Scene 7: Leaving the Building (Lines 208–212)
**Disposition: REVISE**
- **Keep:** The physical description of leaving — the light draining, the gorge, the mental exhaustion.
- **Change:** This should now carry two weeks of accumulated weight, not one day. The exhaustion should be specific — legs that have learned the ramp, a mind that has absorbed a whole new vocabulary, hands callused from carrying.

### Scene 8: The Ryn Scene (Lines 213–327)
**Disposition: KEEP (with small adjustments)**
- This is the chapter's emotional core and the strongest writing. Keep it nearly intact.
- **Adjustments needed:**
  - When Ash tells Ryn about the job, he now has two weeks of material — failures and successes, not just Day One triumphs. His telling should include the embarrassments (standing at locked doors, the misfiled document) alongside the wins. This makes the conversation richer and more honest.
  - The moment where Ryn asks "Is it going to be okay?" should now carry the weight of specific struggles the reader has witnessed. Not abstract anxiety — concrete fears grounded in scenes we've seen.
  - The food-buying-without-calculating beat should land harder because we've now seen him count coins and skip lunch earlier in the chapter. This is an **arrival**, not just a detail.
  - The final reflection about happiness being "unperformed" works better after a chapter where we've watched him perform for two weeks. Keep it.

### Scene 9: Home and Sleep (Lines 298–328)
**Disposition: REVISE**
- **Keep:** The home arrival, cold rice, unlacing boots. The reflection on the Kharren Fault, on Dorenne's nod, on Ryn's laugh.
- **Change:** The "asset identified" nod comparison should feel sharper after we've seen more of Dorenne's pattern — she's had two weeks to show her ledger-thinking, and Ash is starting to notice without quite naming it.

## Notes for Writing Agents

- **Rule #9 (Growth Realism) is the primary constraint.** Every competence Ash demonstrates must be earned through shown experience. Every failure must precede a success.
- The existing Ryn scene (lines 213–327) should be preserved as closely as possible — it's the anchor. Revisions to it should only adjust what Ash *tells* Ryn (now covering two weeks, not one day) and strengthen the food-buying beat with the setup of earlier coin-counting.
- Tessaly is not a warm character. She expresses care through efficiency, scheduling, and the absence of comment. Never through praise.
- Dorenne should feel present but not central in Week One. She's watching, not guiding. Her attention becomes more pointed in Week Two.
- The channeled-lock problem is a **recurring motif**, not a single scene. It should appear enough times that the reader feels the cumulative weight of navigating a world not built for you.
