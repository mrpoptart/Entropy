# Revision Brief — Chapter 10: "Useful at Last"

## Critical Issues (must fix)

**1. "Kilojoules" unit in Dorenne's dialogue**
Dorenne cites "three thousand kilojoules per day" and "ten thousand to bring it back online." The magic system docs explicitly flag kilojoule as an author measurement tool — in-world characters don't use this term. Replace with in-world equivalents: crew-shift equivalents, informal descriptions ("more than any three crews we can field"), or a named in-world unit. The numbers are accurate; the unit is the problem.

**2. Drift from family/workshop arrives too early**
The closing montage has Ash consciously narrating his drift — "He stopped visiting the family as often... He stopped thinking about the workshop. Not a decision. A consequence." The outline assigns the drift arc to Chapters 11–12, with Ch11 still showing family scenes and Ch12 as the point where "old life falls away." Here Ash is aware of and naming the drift in the same chapter the deployments begin — which means Chs 11–12 have nothing left to dramatize. Either cut this section or soften it to behavioral observation without Ash naming the pattern.

**3. Transit shaft mechanism inconsistency with Chapter 1**
Ch1 describes transit lifts as "press your palm to the interface stone, feed it a pulse." Ch10 describes the Meridian Street shaft as requiring "channeling downward through their feet, the reactive force pushing the platform up." These may describe different shaft types, but no distinction is made and it reads as a contradiction. Reconcile.

**4. Grayspire structural channeling: unearned competence**
Ash presses his palms to the foundation and immediately reads the building's stress architecture — feels "a void in the force network," distinguishes sound from failing sections, channels "upward, following the load-bearing walls" on first contact. Nothing in Chs 8–9 established this skill. This is the chapter's clearest wunderkind problem. Fix: show him proceeding without structural fluency. He pushes blind, something goes wrong (a groan, a wrong response), Verant or the engineers give him information. He tries again, finds the path by feel on the third attempt. The building still settles — but earned, not innate.

**5. Closing line overstates identity fusion**
"He was useful. He would do anything to stay this way." — This is the chapter's thesis delivered as plain text, after the chapter has already shown it conclusively through: the smile he didn't decide to have, the gear casing he doesn't notice, the family visits he's already stopping, the map of fixable things. Three reviewers flagged this as closing down emotional resonance the images already built. Multiple options: (a) end on the first open window image currently buried in the intersection scene — "A face appeared, looked up at the sky, disappeared. The window stayed open. The first open window in the eastern district in four months." (b) cut to "He walked through the streets and the streets knew him now" and stop before the thesis. The line is true; it doesn't need to be stated.

## Important Suggestions (should address)

**6. "He stopped checking" framed as active decision**
Line 325: "He stopped checking." The plan describes the drift from tracking cost as passive consequence of feeling abundance — he never properly started, not that he started and stopped. This matters for the math scene's integrity: the reckoning in Act Three depends on Ash not having avoided the question but having genuinely not thought to ask it. Reframe as passive: the reservoir stopped presenting itself as a question worth asking, the way you stop counting the water in a river.

**7. Improvement montage is told, not shown**
"He was getting better... waste heat lower... fewer surges, fewer corrections." The plan's vision for this chapter is "raw practice, hour after hour" — which the reader needs to witness, not be told about. Fix: seed one dramatized self-correction mid-chapter. During a middle junction, the output begins to climb and Ash catches it himself before anyone stops him. ~100 words. That single witnessed beat does more than the full summary paragraph.

**8. Social drift narrated, not dramatized**
Four sentences declare the drift instead of showing it. The drift is one of the most important thematic movements of Book One — if it's asserted here, the consequences in later chapters (Haran's workbench, Ryn's silence) feel externally imposed rather than earned. One small scene: Ash receives a message from his mother asking him to dinner. He reads it at the end of a deployment day. He goes over the next day's schedule with Dorenne instead. He sends a message: next time. The gear casing can appear here. He doesn't look at it.

**9. "The asset she was managing" — too self-aware for this arc position**
Line 373: Ash internally labels himself as "the asset she was managing." That clinical self-categorization belongs to Act Three's reckoning, not the honeymoon phase. He should understand what she's doing without using Dorenne's institutional vocabulary about himself. Find framing through mutual interest rather than control categories.

**10. "She was building a dataset" — redundant label**
Line 139: The preceding action (Dorenne making notes all afternoon, notation system he doesn't recognize) already communicates this. The editorial tag does the reader's work for them. Cut it or replace with a more specific observation from Ash's POV (what does the page look like? what is she tracking?).

**11. Dorenne's dataset fully operational on Day 1**
The outline assigns Dorenne's systematic tracking charts to Chapter 13 ("Dorenne's Architecture"). Having her running a formalized notation system on the first deployment collapses two phases of her evolution. Soften to something more nascent — a few notes, personal shorthand, not a structured system.

**12. Relocate the chapter's emotional terminus**
"He would do anything to stay this way" lands at approximately the chapter's midpoint, with the water system, Grayspire, Meridian Street shaft, montage, and Council scene still to come. The line has the weight of a final image — then the reader's processing is interrupted by Torath warning about pipe joints. Either move this beat to the actual close, or revise the solo walk so it deposits the 23-year accounting without landing the ominous conclusion — let that emerge at the end.

**13. Grayspire scene is 1/3 too long**
The structural channeling sequence overstays relative to its unique contribution. The return visit ("Yes. He went back. Pressed his palms to the stone. Pushed until the engineers said stop.") has correct compression; the initial hour of load-path reading needs trimming. Also: the arms-ache note (physical fatigue from pressing stone for an hour) needs clarification — he's been pressing palms to junction boxes all day; why does Grayspire produce different fatigue?

**14. Retaining wall limitation buried in summary**
"The cracks didn't close — that would have required reshaping the stone, a precision he didn't have." This is the most interesting piece of new information in the chapter's second half: he can reinforce but not reshape, stabilize but not heal. It deserves a scene. The moment where Ash expected the cracks to close, pressed harder, and they didn't — that is a scene, not a footnote.

**15. Triple seeding of "he stopped checking" — reduce to two**
The reservoir-ignorance beat is planted three times in the closing montage: "He stopped checking" / "the ocean didn't notice" / "knowing something wasn't infinite and feeling it as infinite were different things. And the feeling won." The third crosses into authorial commentary and slightly pre-empts Ch19's gut-punch. Cut or soften the third instance.

**16. Final Dorenne office scene needs one beat belonging to Ash**
The scene belongs entirely to Dorenne's agenda (Council session, political capital, Thornwall aqueduct). Ash is receiving. The gear casing / aqueduct map close is good but passive. One moment where Ash's interiority surfaces — even briefly — would give the scene its own weight rather than functioning purely as a logistics vehicle.

**17. Verant's reappearance at Grayspire unexplained**
Verant is introduced at the eastern heating grid; Torath runs the water system with different engineers; then Verant "had joined the rotation" at Grayspire without setup. One sentence anchoring her reassignment closes the gap.

## Polish Notes (nice to have)

- **"The involuntary response of a body that had found the thing it was looking for"** (line 291) — cut. The preceding clause ("the one that happened to his face without his permission") does the work; the explanatory phrase weakens it.
- **"Calibrated distances, the way the light from the high window fell across her desk and left visitors in partial shadow"** — over-description in the opening. "A room built for a person who managed things" can stand alone.
- **"Sloping gently upward"** — trim "gently."
- **"Two objects from two worlds, separated by a layer of fabric"** — authorial interpretation; the image of map against gear casing already does this work. Cut the glossing sentence; keep "He didn't notice the weight of either one."
- **"Reaching places that had been cold for so long he'd stopped noticing the temperature"** — the simile (heat into stone) is earned; this subordinate clause explains what it means. Cut it and let the reader apply the image.
- **"His efficiency rate"** — technical vocabulary Ash wouldn't use about himself. Rephrase in felt terms.
- **Family thread echo** — the chapter is thorough about the workshop drift (gear casing) but the family drift is only asserted. Consider one small sensory echo — a thought, a face — to keep the family thread alive before Ch11 must carry it.
- **Light motif** — windows going from dark to lit as the district warms is an organic opportunity to develop a core trilogy motif. Currently underused given the chapter's subject matter.
- **"Northridge Terrace"** — not named in worldbuilding docs. Track if it appears again.

## Strengths (what's working — preserve)

- **Eastern district walk, lines 151–181** — the chapter's best passage. "Not architecturally — the buildings still leaned... But the walls were warm." Emotion through structure, no labeling. The old woman in the doorway and "felt something in his chest that had nothing to do with the reservoir" is the chapter's peak and exactly how the style guide asks for it.
- **Child pressing her whole body against the warm wall** — best single image in the chapter. Does the work of a paragraph of exposition about generational poverty in two sentences.
- **Transit shaft rolling sentence** — "some of them stood for a moment at the railing and breathed, and some of them walked away without stopping, and one woman sat down on a bench at the top and didn't move..." Earned lyrical peak, precisely timed.
- **"Essentially nothing" echo** — Ash hearing Haran's voice at junction one and deflecting it is the chapter's best seeded thread. The callback is weighted correctly.
- **Gear casing against aqueduct map** — the chapter's best structural image. "Two objects from two worlds... He didn't notice the weight of either one." (Keep the last sentence; cut the interpretive gloss before it.)
- **Dorenne's opening dialogue** — the quarry comparison is devastating and unanswerable, which is exactly how Dorenne argues. "It wasn't a question. They both knew it wasn't a question."
- **"Not a decision. A consequence."** — the triple beat of the drift construction is formally precise. Protect this.
- **Waste heat as failure** — Ash explicitly naming the waste and connecting it to the quarry, where it didn't matter, is the chapter's best piece of magic system dramatization.
- **"Need, deployment, gratitude, next need" named** — the machine given its name without the characters using it. Correct narrative distance for this beat.
- **Dorenne's map going from red to green** — "He noticed the way you noticed weather: as a fact of the environment, neither good nor bad." Exactly the right register.
