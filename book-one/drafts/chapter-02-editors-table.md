## Analysis

### Hearthkeeper Strengths

- **Emotional texture.** The Hearthkeeper excels at the quiet, human detail: Ash counting coins and choosing the cheapest meal, the precise feeling of standing in front of a locked door, the shame-spiral after the misfiled document. These moments have the unhurried quality of real experience.
- **Tessaly as a character.** In the Hearthkeeper draft, Tessaly emerges as a fully realized person whose language for care is efficiency. The rerouted schedule scene is one of the best new additions — it shows character through action without a single word of dialogue about kindness.
- **The Ryn scene.** The expanded telling — where Ash now shares failures alongside wins — gains significant weight from the Hearthkeeper's earlier investment in those failures. The door-map reveal is a small, devastating moment.
- **Length.** At ~10,100 words, the Hearthkeeper gives each new scene room to breathe. The coin-counting, the Osten first-visit failure, and the physical-toll scenes all benefit from the extra space.

### Hearthkeeper Weaknesses

- **Pacing drag.** Some scenes linger past their natural climax. The "mistake" scene, for example, includes Ash lying in bed replaying the mailbox labels — effective for character, but it slows the chapter's momentum at a point where the reader wants to move forward.
- **Occasional over-narration.** A few passages explain what the reader has already felt. The line about Tessaly's schedule being "kindness because efficiency is Tessaly's language for kindness" is a narrator-intrusion that the scene has already demonstrated.

### Carver Strengths

- **Pacing.** The Carver moves. Every scene arrives, lands its beat, and exits. The Day One → Day Two → Day Three progression feels like being carried forward by the work itself, which mirrors Ash's experience.
- **Trust in the reader.** The Carver almost never explains an emotional beat. The misfiled-document scene is four paragraphs instead of eight, and the shame is more vivid for being unnarrated.
- **Dialogue.** Tessaly's lines are devastating in their economy. "That's going to slow us down." / "Right." / "Mm." Each one does the work of a paragraph.
- **Physical precision.** The body tells the story — calves seizing, hands shaking, the specific weight of folios carried.

### Carver Weaknesses

- **Compression risk.** At ~4,200 words, the Carver covers two weeks in less space than the original covered one day. Some of the new scenes (particularly the Days Two through Four block) feel like a montage when they could be scenes. The coin-counting, for example, gets two sentences where it could benefit from a beat more.
- **Ryn scene.** The compression means the Ryn scene arrives with slightly less earned weight. The reader needs to have *felt* the two weeks, and at this word count, some of the feeling is outsourced to the reader's inference.
- **Emotional interiority.** The Carver occasionally under-serves Ash's interior life. His reaction to the misfiled document — "He didn't eat. He lay in the dark" — is powerful in its restraint but might not give enough to a reader who needs to understand why this mistake hits so much harder for someone in Ash's position.

## Recommendation

**Use the Hearthkeeper draft as the base, with targeted Carver-style tightening.**

The Hearthkeeper solves the revision's central problem more completely: it replaces exposition with *lived experience*, giving each failure and each small victory the space it needs to accumulate into the emotional payoff of the Ryn scene. The warmth and interiority are essential to a chapter that's fundamentally about what it feels like to show up every day in a world that wasn't built for you.

However, the Hearthkeeper benefits from the Carver's discipline in specific areas:

1. **Tighten the "mistake" aftermath.** Cut the lyingin-bed-replaying-mailbox-labels paragraph. End the scene with Dorenne handing him the next task.
2. **Trim the narrator-explanations.** Scenes that have already demonstrated their meaning don't need the narration to explain them. Remove lines like "efficiency is Tessaly's language for kindness."
3. **Sharpen Tessaly's lines.** Where the Hearthkeeper gives Tessaly three words, use two. Where it gives her two sentences, use one.
4. **Use Carver's Aldren aftermath.** The Carver's version — "Were you sure?" / "No." — is cleaner than the Hearthkeeper's. The relief reads more vividly in fewer words.

**Estimated final word count after tightening:** ~8,500–9,500 words.

---

## Chapter Split Assessment

At ~9,000 words, this chapter is long but not unwieldy. **Recommendation: keep as a single Chapter 2** with clear section breaks at the day transitions. The emotional arc (uncertainty → failure → accumulation → earned competence → Ryn) is a single through-line that benefits from being held together. Splitting at Week 1/Week 2 would separate the buildup from the payoff.
