# Revision Brief — Chapter 21: "The Break"

## Critical Issues (must fix)

**1. kJ units in dialogue — three instances**
Lines 43, 67, 319: Ash speaks in kJ notation in dialogue and close narration. `magic-system.md` is explicit: kilojoules are the author's measurement system; in-world characters do not use this term. Fix: convert to in-world relative framing — percentage of remaining pool, elder-day equivalents, or unit-free comparative language. This is the project's most persistent worldbuilding violation and has been flagged across chapters 14, 15, 16, 19, 20, and 21. (World Keeper — critical; recurring flag.)

**2. Reservoir math contradicts continuity tracking**
The chapter's implicit math leaves ~22M remaining (~44% of Wellspring), but the outline tracks ~50% remaining at this point. The discrepancy is approximately 6M — significant enough to affect the book's climactic math. Fix: reconcile the chapter's expenditure figures with the outline's tracking number. Either the chapter's spending figures must decrease or the outline's running total must be updated with a documented rationale. (World Keeper — critical arithmetic error; Plot Sync Rule applies.)

**3. Constituency-bias accusation is unearned — shifts break's character**
The deployment schedule bias accusation (Dorenne favoring wealthier districts in scheduling) arrives in the confrontation without prior dramatic grounding. The outline designates the break as "both people right and both wrong" — a tragedy of two people unable to hold the full picture simultaneously. As written, the accusation reframes the break as grievance (Ash has a specific, articulable complaint) rather than tragedy (two legitimate frameworks failing to coexist). Fix: either plant the constituency-favoritism observation in Ch18 or Ch19 so it arrives in Ch21 as a recognized pattern, not a new accusation; or revise the confrontation so Ash's challenge is the more fundamental one — not "your scheduling favors the wealthy" but "I am not an instrument." The accusation can still be present as a symptom, but the break's core must be identity, not policy. (Arc Guardian — critical; Plot Sync: needs outline.md update to Chapter 21 entry per CLAUDE.md instructions. Convergence Tracker concurs — accusation has no convergence map entry.)

**4. Final paragraph is a POV violation — Ash cannot see Dorenne's desk**
The closing image shows Dorenne's desk with Ash's notation on it after Ash has left the room. Third-person limited means Ash's physical position constrains what the narration can access. This is the chapter's most explicit POV breach and also provides closure the chapter has been deliberately withholding — it resolves the final beat in the reader's favor, undercutting the chapter's hard-won ambiguity. Fix: cut or revise the final paragraph so the chapter ends on Ash's last perception before leaving, not on Dorenne's desk after. (Voice Editor — critical POV violation; Pace Keeper concurs — the paragraph provides closure that should be withheld.)

**5. Confrontation is too composed — Ash's history as a people-pleaser not honored**
Ash has spent 21 chapters accommodating, deflecting, and absorbing rather than confronting. The break scene has him arrive composed and leave composed, with the only movement being one line's stumble. A man with this history would hesitate, fumble, possibly begin to accommodate before catching himself. The absence of this stumble makes the confrontation read as a character the reader hasn't met performing an act the Ash they know couldn't yet perform without visible cost. Fix: let Ash begin to comply — the familiar reflex, a word of agreement forming — before something stops him. The recovery is the break; the stumble earns it. (Pace Keeper — critical character arc issue; Arc Guardian concurs.)

## Important Suggestions (should address)

**6. Narrator over-explains after "I am not a water main"**
The line lands. The paragraph following it analyzes why it lands — the repair register, the infrastructure language, what it means that Ash has adopted Dorenne's framing. The line is doing this work without the analysis. Fix: cut the explanatory paragraph following the "water main" line. End the beat on the silence after the line is spoken. (Voice Editor.)

**7. Extended bench meditation is at wrong narrative distance**
The bench scene's middle passage runs in analytical mode — frameworks being named, the trap being categorized — at a distance that belongs to an essay, not to a man sitting outside watching the city. The chapter's best material works through sensation and image, not through framework analysis. Fix: compress the analytical passage by half; replace the named-framework sentences with the physical experience of sitting on a bench with something broken. The bench's one concrete object (currently missing) should anchor the scene. (Voice Editor; Arc Guardian — bench needs one concrete grounding object.)

**8. Overlook sequence is doing Chapter 22's work**
The overlook sequence (estimated 900 words post-Ryn junction) provides two comfortable stopping places and reaches a thesis resolution that the outline assigns to Ch22's workshop ending. Convergence zone chapters must not resolve; they must hold. Fix: cut the overlook sequence from the junction scene onward. Ash arrives at the overlook, looks at the city — one paragraph — and the chapter ends. The thesis, the resolution, the understanding belongs to Ch22. What Ch21 ends on is the fact of a broken thing and the city still below him. (Story Architect — highest structural priority; Convergence Tracker concurs.)

**9. Ryn appears too late — should precede corridor processing**
Currently Ryn's junction moment comes after Ash has already processed the confrontation through corridor interiority and begun to recompose. The convergence map designates her appearance as catching Ash before he recomposes — her value as a convergence beat is that she sees him still raw. Fix: move Ryn earlier in the post-office sequence so the junction moment interrupts his processing rather than arriving after it is complete. (Convergence Tracker.)

**10. Thread density too low for convergence zone position**
Chapter 21 fires only 4 active threads against a convergence zone target of 6–10. Threads 2 (Infrastructure) and 5 (Efficiency) are present in the chapter's material but not activated as threads — they are used as scene texture without being brought to narrative foreground. Fix: in the office confrontation, let Ash's awareness of the dimming maintenance lattices land as Thread 2 convergence, not just scene texture; in the bench scene, let one beat explicitly connect Ash's efficiency level to the remaining timeline. Both are materially present; neither requires new scenes. (Convergence Tracker.)

**11. Corridor/entrance-hall passage restates without advancing**
The interiority between the office and Ryn restates the confrontation's emotional content without moving forward or deepening. It is a holding passage — summarizing what just happened for readers who might have lost it. Cut or compress by two-thirds; the chapter's pacing in the convergence zone cannot sustain summary. (Story Architect.)

**12. Opening walk is over-long**
The opening walk sequence should be trimmed by approximately 300 words. The convergence zone requires scene compression; the opening currently runs at a pace appropriate for act one. Keep the opening's best image (the stone-in-pocket callback) and cut the rest to arrive at the office scene faster. (Story Architect.)

**13. "I'm not a water main" needs the stumble before it**
Per issue 5, but specifically: the confrontation's best line needs the failed-compliance attempt to precede it. Without the stumble, the line reads as a prepared statement. With it, the line reads as a discovery — the moment Ash hears himself say something he didn't plan to say. (Pace Keeper, Arc Guardian.)

**14. Dorenne described as channeling with "elder" efficiency — generation error**
"With the invisible efficiency of an elder channeler" — Dorenne is Established generation (late 50s), not Elder generation. The phrasing implies a generational pool-size that contradicts her established profile. Fix: "with the efficiency of someone who had spent forty years not wasting anything" or similar — anchors in practice, not generation. (World Keeper.)

**15. Ryn's singed patch of floor — missed thematic resonance**
The singed patch is the chapter's strongest motif seed but is treated as scene texture. A half-beat in the junction passage where Ash registers the patch as something he recognizes — not named, but felt — would arm the image for Ch22's use. (Thematic Compass.)

**16. Dimming lattices should return on the descent**
The maintenance lattice images established in the opening walk should echo on the descent from the overlook (or wherever the chapter ends post-Ryn). The visual motif of dimming infrastructure needs to bracket the chapter, not just open it. (Thematic Compass.)

**17. Maintenance crew woman — missed Maren resonance**
The woman from the maintenance crew (if she appears in Ch21) should carry a half-beat of Maren recognition — precise, disciplined work with limited tools. One sentence; quickly submerged. The cross-thread amplification between Thread 8 (Maren's Parallel Decline) and the maintenance crew is designated in the convergence map. (Thematic Compass.)

**18. "Both people right and both wrong" — confrontation must preserve this**
The outline's characterization of the break requires the confrontation to be genuinely ambiguous — Ash's position and Dorenne's position must both be correct and both be insufficient. As revised to address issue 3, confirm that Dorenne's "What would you have me do?" lands as a real question, not a rhetorical defense. She is not wrong. He is not wrong. The break is the inability to hold both truths simultaneously. (Arc Guardian.)
