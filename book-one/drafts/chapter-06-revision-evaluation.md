## 3. Criterion-by-Criterion Analysis

### Voice & Style

**Carver (9/10).** Tightest voice match to chapters 4-5. Exact compliance with X-as-Y reduction. Declarative clarity maintained throughout. The training paragraph ("Progress measured in things not broken. A strange metric, but his.") is signature Carver.

**Hearthkeeper (8/10).** Strong throughout but slightly exceeds X-as-Y limit. "The architecture of a system" and the mason-keystone simile add constructions beyond the brief's 2-3 cap. Warmer tone suits family scenes but occasionally crosses into narrator-as-commentator territory ("heavy as stone" after the father's devastating line).

### Emotional Impact

**Hearthkeeper (9/10).** Three advantages: (1) Father's kitchen scene is the best version across all drafts — both the "heating lattice" observation AND "being useful" line give the father two registers. (2) Demonstration scene is more immersive — the "spring arriving" simile renders warmth from the audience's perspective. (3) Dorenne's post-demo speech lands practical implications.

**Carver (8/10).** The father's Hall moment ("Did you know I was proud of you before this?") is a devastating beat the Hearthkeeper drops entirely — significant loss. The training paragraph earns the demonstration by showing prior struggle. Without it (Hearthkeeper), Ash performs flawlessly, weakening Growth Realism.

### Pacing & Structure

**Carver (8/10).** Five family beats, each serving a distinct function. Q&A compression is more aggressive, creating clean momentum. The chapter moves well.

**Hearthkeeper (7/10).** Four family beats, but the demonstration scene runs longer and the father's kitchen scene expands. Net effect: more words in the Hall and kitchen, less efficient transitions.

### Character

**Hearthkeeper (9/10).** Dorenne is sharper with "Presentable/Remarkable." Father is fuller with both lines. Maren placed first in aftermath gives her more weight. Ryn's "Did it cost you much?" is an excellent exclusive beat.

**Carver (8/10).** "Your mother is your mother" is the chapter's most compressed character work. The father's Hall moment (shoulder grip, look back, italicized question) is the strongest exclusive Carver passage. Dorenne coaching is competent but less witty without "Presentable/Remarkable."

### Worldbuilding Integration

**Hearthkeeper (9/10).** The demonstration "before" passage connects directly to Thread 2 without exposition. Dorenne's post-demo speech reinforces through character voice. Most immersive lattice activation across all drafts.

**Carver (8/10).** Efficient worldbuilding-through-observation. Slightly less immersive demonstration — good technical specificity but less emotionally resonant.

### Continuity & Seeds

**Carver (9/10).** All four threads progressed correctly. Critically, Thread 11's resentment seed is preserved — "it didn't just happen to him. It was him." This is essential for the convergence map.

**Hearthkeeper (7/10).** Thread 11 resentment seed is DROPPED. After "Ryn's version framed it as weather," the text skips directly to "He didn't say any of this." The outline explicitly requires "He appreciates this and also, faintly, resents it." This is a significant omission that will cause problems when the Ryn tension needs to escalate in later chapters. Threads 6, 9, and 12 are well-handled.
