# Editor's Table — Chapter 1 Revision Comparison

## Drafts Under Review
- 📜 **Carver** — structural precision focus
- 🔥 **Hearthkeeper** — emotional deepening focus

---

## What Both Drafts Do Well

Both drafts successfully execute the three core revisions from the brief:

1. **Restructured Dorenne encounter.** Both versions have Ash step onto the lift platform and press his palm to the interface. Both render the silence — the dead stone under his hand while her side hums. Both preserve the ramp, the conversation, and the handshake verbatim.

2. **Deepened stairwell pause.** Both add physical sensation to the emotional release — jaw unclenching, shoulders dropping, the body acknowledging what the performance costs.

3. **Reworked Maren's silenced achievement.** Both add the Thornwall survey specificity and make the pivot back to Ash feel organic rather than scripted.

All preserved passages from the brief survive in both drafts. The chapter's best lines remain intact.

## Key Differences

### The Lift Failure

| | 📜 Carver | 🔥 Hearthkeeper |
|---|---|---|
| **Setup** | Dorenne asks "Coming up?" — casual, assumes he'll follow | Dorenne asks "Going up?" — same casualness |
| **The moment** | Clean and external: "Nothing." One-word paragraph. Moves quickly to his practiced exit. | Lingers inside Ash's head: "He stood there for a breath longer than he needed to." Adds the history — six, seven, eight, the counting he stopped doing. Dwells on what the moment *asks* of him. |
| **Her response** | She takes her hand off the interface and looks at him "the way she might look at a structural report that had an unexpected number in it." | She lifts her hand and looks at him "with the direct attention of someone who'd encountered a problem outside her model." |
| **Tone** | Fast, clean, architectural. The humiliation is implicit. | Slower, interior, more felt. The humiliation is rendered from inside. |

**Assessment:** The Carver version is tighter and trusts the reader more. The Hearthkeeper version is more emotionally specific — the paragraph about "the moment always asked something of him" adds a dimension that the Carver doesn't attempt. Both work. The choice depends on whether you want the lift scene to move at the speed of *action* (Carver) or at the speed of *experience* (Hearthkeeper).

### The Stairwell Pause

| | 📜 Carver | 🔥 Hearthkeeper |
|---|---|---|
| **Physical detail** | Adds: "His shoulders dropped... His hands uncurled in his lap. He could feel the tendons in his forearms releasing one at a time, like lines being let off a dock." | Adds: "The set of his shoulders loosened, and he felt the ache where he'd been holding them... His jaw unclenched. His hands opened in his lap, palms up, as if they were waiting for something to be placed in them." |
| **Approach** | Nautical metaphor (lines being let off a dock). Precise, slightly more external. | The hands "palms up, as if waiting for something to be placed in them" — a gesture of involuntary openness. More vulnerable. |

**Assessment:** Both add the physical anchor the brief requested. The Hearthkeeper's "palms up, as if waiting for something to be placed in them" is the more evocative image — it adds meaning beyond the physical, suggesting an unconscious openness he doesn't permit himself elsewhere. The Carver's "lines being let off a dock" is cleaner but less emotionally layered.

### Maren's Silenced Achievement

| | 📜 Carver | 🔥 Hearthkeeper |
|---|---|---|
| **The achievement** | "highest in cohort" framing, plus "They're using my data as the baseline for the reshoring schedule" | Same reshoring detail, but adds a physical gesture: "She traced a circle on the table with her fingertip, a small gesture that looked idle and wasn't." |
| **The pivot** | Mother asks about clothes and logistics; the conversation drifts to Ash mechanically. | Ash himself causes the pivot — he hears "eastern terrace," connects it to the posting board, and starts thinking out loud about aqueducts. The narrator notes: "He didn't mean to redirect." |
| **Effect** | The silencing is the family's unconscious pattern. Ash is passive in it. | The silencing is partly Ash's doing — his excited mind pulls the conversation toward his new world. He's complicit without knowing it. |

**Assessment:** The Hearthkeeper version is substantially stronger here. Making Ash complicit in the silencing — without cruelty, without intent, just through the natural momentum of his excitement — is truer to the character profile, which says he's "capable of willful blindness when hope is on the line." It makes the Maren thread sharper because the reader sees the mechanism: it's not that the family ignores Maren, it's that Ash's gravity is so strong right now that everything bends toward him, and he doesn't notice because he's never been the gravitational center before.

### Other Differences

**Morning routine:** The Hearthkeeper retains the sentence about every child learning to warm a lattice by five. The Carver cuts it. Both choices are defensible — the line is slightly expository but also lands as a specific detail of Ash's exclusion.

**Mother's late-night expression:** The Carver revises "the late-night expression, after the channeling was done" into a longer, more specific image. The Hearthkeeper keeps the original phrasing. The Carver version is slightly better — more visual, less abstract.

**Closing image:** Both add physical sensation to the final window scene. The Carver adds "A looseness behind his ribs. The ability to breathe one degree deeper than usual." The Hearthkeeper leaves the closing as-is, relying on the forehead-to-glass detail. The Carver's addition is better — it gives the closing beat a physical anchor.

---

## Recommendation

**Use the Hearthkeeper as primary, with Carver patches in three places:**

1. **Lift scene:** Use the Hearthkeeper's interior version but tighten the "He stood there for a breath" paragraph by one sentence — cut "that hope had died when he was six, and seven, and eight, and eventually at some age he'd stopped counting." This is backstory that the reader doesn't need yet and slightly over-explains. The Carver's momentum is right; the Hearthkeeper's interiority is right. Split the difference.

2. **Mother's expression:** Use the Carver's more specific rendering of the late-night face.

3. **Closing image:** Use the Carver's added physical anchor ("A looseness behind his ribs. The ability to breathe one degree deeper than usual").

The Hearthkeeper's Maren pivot — where Ash unintentionally redirects the conversation — is the strongest single revision in either draft and should be preserved exactly as written.
